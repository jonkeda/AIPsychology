# Where is your team? — A quick AI adoption diagnostic

## How to use this worksheet

This worksheet helps you gauge where your team stands in the AI adoption process. You can fill it in on your own as a quick self-scan, or talk it through with your team as a starting point for an honest conversation. Be candid — the answers are for your own insight, not for a report. This isn't a scored test. It's a practical tool to spot where the energy is, where the friction is, and what a sensible next step might look like.

## Seven statements — where does your team recognise itself?

Read the statements below and tick the ones you recognise. Recognising several at once is perfectly normal — people in your team are rarely all in the same phase.

1. **"AI might be interesting for other teams, but it's not really relevant for what we do."**
   → Dismissal — AI isn't on the radar yet. There's no active pushback, but no curiosity either.

2. **"I gave it a go, but it produces more rubbish than anything useful."**
   → Resistance — There's been some experimentation, but expectations were too high or the task too complex. The verdict is already in.

3. **"If AI can do this in thirty seconds, what are we even here for?"**
   → The Hit — The confrontation with what AI can do strikes at identity and self-worth. This is a vulnerable phase.

4. **"I use it for first drafts, but the real substance — that's always me."**
   → The Deal — A workable division is taking shape, but with firm boundaries. Control stays emphatically with the human.

5. **"I want to systematically test when it's good enough before I rely on it."**
   → Building Trust — The emotion has faded, the nuance arrives. There's a deliberate evaluation of what works and what doesn't.

6. **"Why would we still spend time on this manually when AI can handle it?"**
   → Understanding and Overcompensation — Enthusiasm is high, but critical judgement lags behind. Quality risk.

7. **"It's just a tool — like email or Excel. Sometimes useful, sometimes not."**
   → Stabilisation — AI is woven into the work without fanfare. Watch out: this can also tip into standstill.

### What do the results tell you?

The statements you recognise most strongly point to your team's dominant phase. But keep in mind: within a single team, people can sit in different phases. That's not a problem — that's reality. The trick is to tailor your approach to the spread, not to force everyone into the same mould.

## Bonus question: emotional or practical?

Not all resistance is the same. Listen carefully to *what* people are actually saying:

- Are the complaints about what AI **means**? ("We'll be redundant soon", "This doesn't feel right") → The resistance is **emotional**. Facts and training won't help here — acknowledgement and conversation will.
- Are the complaints about what AI **does** or fails to do? ("The output is wrong", "It costs me more time than it saves") → The resistance is **practical**. Better prompts, different tasks, or more realistic expectations will help here.

The wrong intervention for the wrong type makes things worse. Someone wrestling with existential doubt won't benefit from a prompt workshop. Someone getting poor results won't benefit from a chat about feelings.
