## Results — What does your score tell you?

Your highest-scoring block maps to one of these phases:

### Block A → Dismissal — "AI isn't relevant to me"

You haven't engaged with AI yet, and you don't feel a pressing need to. This isn't laziness — it often means AI simply hasn't touched your daily work in a visible way. **What helps:** low-stakes exposure. Try one small task with an AI tool and see what happens. The [playbook](playbook.md) has concrete suggestions.

### Block B → Resistance — "It's not good enough"

You've tried AI and found it wanting. You're holding it to high standards — often your own expert standards — and it's not meeting them. That's a valid observation, but it can also keep you from seeing where AI *does* add value. **What helps:** reframing AI as a rough-draft tool rather than a finished-product tool. See the [playbook](playbook.md) for practical approaches.

### Block C → The Hit / Ego Shock — "What's my role now?"

AI doing something well that you're proud of has shaken you. This is the most personal phase — it touches your professional identity. It's uncomfortable, and it's completely normal. **What helps:** acknowledging the feeling out loud and separating "AI can do X" from "I'm no longer valuable." The [playbook](playbook.md) has specific exercises for this.

### Block D → The Deal / Bargaining — "Only for X"

You've found a way to coexist with AI by drawing clear lines: AI does this, I do that. It feels manageable, but the lines can become rigid. Watch for defensiveness when someone suggests moving the boundary. **What helps:** consciously revisiting your boundaries every few weeks. The [playbook](playbook.md) shows you how.

### Block E → Building Trust — "Let me test first"

You're in exploration mode — comparing, testing, calibrating. This is a productive phase. You're building your own evidence base for when to use AI and when not to. **What helps:** keeping notes on your experiments and sharing findings with colleagues. The [playbook](playbook.md) offers a lightweight testing framework.

### Block F → Understanding and Overcompensation — "Use it for everything!"

You've swung to the other side — AI is brilliant and you want to maximise it. The risk here is losing your own judgement or quality standards. Watch for the moment you stop checking AI output critically. **What helps:** deliberately doing some tasks without AI to maintain your own skills. The [playbook](playbook.md) explains when to pull back.

### Block G → Stabilisation — "Just a tool"

AI has found its place in your workflow. You use it where it helps, skip it where it doesn't, and you don't think about it much anymore. This is the target state — but stay alert for new capabilities that might shift the balance. **What helps:** staying curious without feeling pressure. The [playbook](playbook.md) covers how to maintain this balance.

## How to read your results

- **Highest block = your likely dominant phase.** That's where most of your energy around AI is going right now.
- **Two blocks tied?** You're in transition between phases — that's normal. Nobody moves in a straight line.
- **Multiple high blocks?** You're processing several things at once. Also normal. AI adoption isn't a neat staircase.
- **This is a conversation starter, not a diagnosis.** Use it to understand yourself better, not to label yourself.
