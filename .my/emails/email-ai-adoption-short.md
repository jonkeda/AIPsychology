## The cycle in 60 seconds

People don't adopt AI rationally. They go through an emotional sequence:

**0. Pre-Exposure**
Most people start here. AI simply isn't on their radar. No opinion, no reaction, no relevance.

**1. Dismissal**
"It won't work." A protective stance — people dismiss AI to avoid confronting what it might mean for their skills and identity.

**2. Defensive Resistance**
"I tried it. It got X wrong. See?" People look for flaws to feel safe. This isn't objective evaluation — it's ego defence.

**3. Ego Shock**
"If it can do all this… what am I worth?" The phase nobody talks about. When defences collapse, people face anxiety, grief, and fear of obsolescence.

**3B. Bargaining**
"I'll use it for drafts, but the real work is mine." People negotiate partial adoption to preserve their sense of competence. Many stay here for months.

**4. Trust Evaluation**
"Let me test this properly before I commit." Rational assessment, not emotional resistance. Not all caution is fear.

**5. Understanding**
"It's powerful but limited. I can work with this." The first stable phase. But it requires social normalisation too — not just personal reframing.

**6. Overcompensation**
"I want to use it for everything." The pendulum swings too far. Can be driven by enthusiasm, anxiety, efficiency pressure, or peer pressure.

**7. Stabilisation**
People settle into maturity, identity expansion, evangelism — or burnout. Multiple end states, not one destination. And every major model release can loop people back.

Not everyone hits every stage. But the emotional ones — resistance, ego shock, bargaining — are where adoption *actually* stalls. And every major model release can loop people back.

---

## What this means for us

1. **Mandating AI without emotional readiness creates hidden resistance.** People comply but disengage.
2. **Your loudest resisters are often your best future champions.** High-skill people resist hardest because they have the most to lose — and the most to gain.
3. **Bargaining ("I'll use it only for X") is where most people will sit for months.** That's fine. Don't rush it.
4. **Not all resistance is emotional.** Sometimes the tool just doesn't work well enough. Diagnose before intervening.
5. **This isn't a one-off rollout.** Every capability jump resets the cycle. Plan for ongoing support.
