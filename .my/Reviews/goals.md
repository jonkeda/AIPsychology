# Project Goals — AI Psychology Model

A catalog of possible goals for work on [Model3.md](../Model/Model3.md). Goals are grouped by intent. Each one is a candidate — not all should be pursued, and several conflict with each other (e.g., academic rigor vs. mass-audience accessibility). Use this as a menu when planning the next iteration.

---

## A. Improve the Model Itself

Goals aimed at making v4 (or vN) a better model.

- **A1. Insights to improve the model** — solicit structured critique to find weaknesses, gaps, and overreach. *(See [002-prompts-v3.md](002-prompts-v3.md) and [005-prompts-v4.md](005-prompts-v4.md).)*
- **A2. Falsifiability pass** — convert vague stage descriptions into operational, testable criteria.
- **A3. Mechanism grounding** — anchor each stage to an established psychological construct (self-efficacy, dissonance, threat appraisal, TAM, etc.) instead of standing alone.
- **A4. Dynamics layer** — specify durations, transition speeds, and typical trajectories, not just states.
- **A5. Scope declaration** — explicitly state which populations, contexts, and time periods the model claims to cover.
- **A6. Simplification pass** — identify whether v3 is *over-engineered* and whether a smaller model explains as much.
- **A7. Versioning discipline** — define what counts as a v3.1 patch vs. a v4 rewrite.

---

## B. Validate the Model Empirically

Goals aimed at testing whether the model matches reality.

- **B1. User interview study** — recruit 20–40 people across delegation depths and check whether their narratives map to the stages.
- **B2. Longitudinal observation** — follow a small cohort over 6–12 months to test whether the predicted transitions occur.
- **B3. Survey instrument** — turn stages into a self-report questionnaire and test for construct validity.
- **B4. Cross-cultural validation** — test the cultural-variability claims against participants from at least 3 distinct cultural contexts.
- **B5. Adversarial validation** — actively recruit people who *don't* fit the model and document what's missing.
- **B6. Retrospective coding** — code existing interviews, blog posts, or forum threads about AI adoption against the model.

---

## C. Apply the Model

Goals aimed at making the model useful, not just true.

- **C1. Diagnostic tool** — a short assessment that places someone at a likely current stage.
- **C2. Intervention playbook** — for each stage, what helps, what backfires, and what to leave alone.
- **C3. Manager / change-leader guide** — applied version for organizational AI rollouts.
- **C4. Coaching framework** — applied version for 1:1 work with individuals.
- **C5. Educator guide** — for teachers helping students through delegation-depth questions.
- **C6. Self-assessment** — a public-facing version people can use on themselves.
- **C7. Product design implications** — what AI tool builders should do differently for users at each stage.

---

## D. Communicate the Model

Goals aimed at reach and clarity.

- **D1. Plain-language version** — strip the jargon, target a general audience.
- **D2. Visual / diagram version** — a clean state diagram showing stages, transitions, and triggers.
- **D3. Short-form summary** — a one-page or one-tweet distillation.
- **D4. Long-form article** — publishable essay or blog post.
- **D5. Academic paper** — peer-review-ready version with literature review and methods.
- **D6. Talk / presentation** — slide deck for conference or internal talks.
- **D7. Interactive web version** — clickable stages, examples, self-placement.

---

## E. Extend the Model

Goals aimed at scope expansion.

- **E1. Organizational adaptation cycle** — same framework applied to teams and companies, not individuals.
- **E2. Societal / generational layer** — how cohorts (Gen Z native users vs. mid-career adopters) move through the cycle differently.
- **E3. Reverse cycle** — what happens when people *stop* using AI (regulation, burnout, dependency rejection).
- **E4. Multi-tool generalization** — does the model generalize beyond AI to any disruptive cognitive technology (search engines, calculators, GPS)?
- **E5. AI-side model** — symmetric: how AI systems are "adapted to" by their developers and operators.
- **E6. Relational layer** — how relationships (couples, teams, mentor-mentee pairs) navigate asymmetric adoption.
- **E7. Identity reconstruction layer** — what stable post-Stage-7 identities actually look like, in depth.

---

## F. Compare and Position

Goals aimed at situating the model in the existing landscape.

- **F1. Comparative analysis** — side-by-side with Kübler-Ross, TAM, Rogers, Transtheoretical, Hype Cycle.
- **F2. Originality audit** — explicitly mark what is novel vs. borrowed.
- **F3. Literature review** — comprehensive survey of related work in technology adoption and identity disruption.
- **F4. Competing-models comparison** — contrast with other AI-adoption models that emerge (this space is moving fast in 2026).

---

## G. Meta / Process Goals

Goals about *how* the work is done, not its content.

- **G1. Reviewer panel** — assemble a recurring set of reviewers (LLMs and humans) for each version.
- **G2. Change log discipline** — every revision gets a documented rationale and a diff against the prior version.
- **G3. Open critique repository** — collect critiques publicly and track which were accepted, deferred, or rejected with reasons.
- **G4. Pre-registration** — for empirical work (B-track), pre-register hypotheses derived from the model before collecting data.
- **G5. Conflict-of-interest log** — track the author's own stage in the model over time, since it influences which critiques feel persuasive.

---

## H. Decisions to Make Before Picking Goals

Questions worth answering before committing effort.

- **H1.** Is the primary audience *researchers*, *practitioners*, or *general readers*? (These pull in different directions.)
- **H2.** Is the goal a *better description* or a *useful intervention*? (A good description can be intervention-useless and vice versa.)
- **H3.** Should empirical validation precede or follow further theoretical refinement?
- **H4.** What is the desired half-life of the model — months (will be obsoleted by next AI generation) or years (claims to capture invariants)?
- **H5.** Is the model's value in being *correct* or in being *useful framing* even if imprecise?

---

## Suggested Sequencing (one opinionated path)

1. **A1** (gather critiques) → **A2/A3** (tighten definitions and mechanisms) — produces v3.5.
2. **B6 + B1** (light empirical pass) — sanity check before bigger investment.
3. **D2 + D1** (diagram + plain language) — makes the model communicable to reviewers.
4. **C1 + C2** (diagnostic + intervention) — makes the model useful, which sharpens it further.
5. **F1** (comparative positioning) — only worth doing once the model is stable enough to compare.
6. **B3** (survey instrument) — only after stages are operationalized.

Other sequences are valid; this one prioritizes "tighten, then test, then apply" over "publish quickly."
