# Earn-Its-Keep Audit — Model V5

## TL;DR

- **Cut S0 entirely** (Baseline). No explanatory power, no operational use, no falsifiability. The model is stronger if it starts at S1.
- **Demote the S3B/S7A heuristic distinction** from testable claim to "currently untested." Requires longitudinal observation and self-report; does not meet the falsifiability bar.
- **Delete or move the Cultural Variability section.** Explicitly conjecture with zero cross-cultural data gathered.
- **Demote three other claims** (S5 dual-component claim, S7F S3-prerequisite, frequency of cycling) marked as internal conjecture but not flagged clearly in the main text. Move to "Limits of operationalization."
- **Keep all S1, S2T, S2D, S3, S3B, S5, S6A–D, S7A–F.** Each earns its place on explanatory power, operational use, or falsifiability.
- **Keep both axes, all mechanism anchors, dropout plane, reversibility test, trigger layer, context layer, and Limits section.** These earn their keep.
- **Headline cut count: Delete 1 state (S0), delete or demote 4 claims, simplify 1 heuristic.** V6 is cleaner and more intellectually honest.

## Verdict

V5 is architecturally sound. The core states (S1–S7F), both axes, the mechanism anchors, and the dropout plane all satisfy at least one criterion: explanatory power (predicts outcomes a simpler model misses), operational use (guides intervention differently), or falsifiability (makes testable predictions). The model's honesty about what's measured versus conjectural is a strength. But V5 carries scaffolding that should be cut: the S0 baseline state (tautological and operationally inert), an entirely speculative Cultural Variability section, and three other claims marked internally as conjecture but not clearly labeled as such in main text. Removing S0 and demoting four untested claims to the Limits section yields a leaner, more falsifiable V6.

## Findings

### KEEP (16 Core Constructs)

- **S1 — Initial Encounter.** Novelty appraisal explains the fork at S1 (Identity Stakes determines path). Falsifiable and explains why people diverge.

- **S2T — Trust Evaluation.** Calibration under uncertainty is falsifiable and operationally actionable. Distinguishes rational testing from ego defense.

- **S2D — Defensive Resistance.** Identity-protection cognition explains asymmetric updating. Intervention differs from S2T: acknowledge the threat rather than marshaling evidence.

- **S3 — Ego Shock / Identity Disruption.** Self-efficacy collapse plus terror management is the emotional core. The Stakes × D-Level table provides strong explanatory power for intensity prediction.

- **S6A — Enthusiastic Overuse.** Variable-reward reinforcement explains compulsive engagement and is falsifiable. Distinct driver from S6B/C/D; requires different interventions (feedback on misuse, not confidence-rebuilding).

- **S6B — Dependent Overuse.** Self-efficacy displacement (confidence in AI replaces confidence in self) is a distinct, observable failure mode. Rebuild independent judgment alongside use, not instead of use.

- **S6C — Driven Overuse.** Goal substitution (efficiency becomes terminal goal) explains productivity-pressure-driven excess. Requires structural intervention, not just tool feedback.

- **S6D — Social Overuse.** Normative social influence (Asch) is well-established and falsifiable. Explains why the same person adopts at different intensities in different peer groups.

- **S7A — Maturity (Healthy Integration).** Metacognitive calibration (accurate self-assessment of when human judgment beats AI) is falsifiable and distinguishes healthy scoping from defensive scoping.

- **S7B — Identity Expansion.** Tool incorporation into self-concept is documented across domains (hammer and carpenter; camera and photographer). Explains generative adoption and distinct risk profile.

- **S7C — Ethical / Philosophical Integration.** Meaning-making explains sustained reflection without adoption refusal. Rare as sole state; real and important for practitioners to recognize.

- **S7D — Burnout / Withdrawal.** Resource depletion is the right mechanism. Distinguishes from dropout by trajectory: follows high engagement, not early resistance.

- **S7F — Influence / Evangelism.** Generativity (Erikson) explains mentoring-driven identity. Predicts who helps others through the cycle.

- **Both Axes (Identity Stakes, Task Delegation Level).** Stakes predicts fork and intensity; D-level predicts intensity within states. Operationally observable and falsifiable.

- **Dropout as Two-Axis Plane (Practical × Emotional Severity).** Two independent axes explain identical surface behaviors from different causes. Reversibility test provides clean operationalization.

- **Mechanism Anchors, Limits Section, Trigger and Context Layers, Loop Property, ⚡ Convention.** All earn their place by explaining transitions, guiding intervention, or enabling falsification.

### WEAKEN (5 Claims)

- **S3B/S7A Heuristic Distinction.** The model states: "This is a heuristic, not a measured fact. Requires longitudinal observation across multiple model releases, and is contaminated by self-report once a subject has read this model." Explicitly fails falsifiability standard. **Action:** Move to "Limits of operationalization" as testable hypothesis, not model claim.

- **S5 Dual-Component Stability Claim (internal reframing + social normalization).** The model states: "Currently conjecture. No instrument is specified for either component, and any observed discomfort can be attributed to mismatch. Treated here as a hypothesis to test, not a tested claim." **Action:** Rewrite main text to downgrade: "Stability appears to involve internal model coherence and ambient social normalization, but neither is operationalized and the claim is untested."

- **S7F S3-Prerequisite Claim.** The model states: "Most stable when the person has previously passed through S3… the S3 prerequisite claim is currently conjecture." **Action:** Remove from main text; move to "Limits of operationalization" as testable hypothesis ("S3 experience predicts stable S7F").

- **D1–D4 as Sharply Bounded Categories.** The four levels are qualitatively useful but boundaries are intuitive, not empirically defined. Where does "summarize article" end and "write report with guidance" begin? **Action:** Add note that these are rough qualitative buckets, not precise categories.

- **"Most People Cycle Through the Model More Than Once."** The model states: "V5 deliberately avoids numerical claims about cycle frequency." Yet includes a frequency claim without evidence. **Action:** Rewrite to "People can cycle through the model as AI evolves; V5 makes no claims about frequency or prevalence."

### DELETE (2 Constructs)

- **S0 — Baseline.** Fails all three tests. No explanatory power (it's the absence of state). No operational use (practitioners don't assess "at S0?"). No falsifiability (tautological by definition). **Rationale:** The model is stronger if it starts at S1. Replace with: "Entry point is triggered by meaningful AI exposure (demonstration, mandate, peer adoption, media narrative, personal experience)."

- **Cultural Variability Section.** The model states: "This entire section is currently conjecture. No cross-cultural data has been gathered." Speculative on explanatory power, speculative on operational use, untestable as written. **Rationale:** Interesting hypothesis but belongs in "Limits of operationalization" with a research proposal (cross-cultural measurement of state sequence and dwell time), not in V5 main text.

## Recommendations

1. **Delete S0 entirely.** Rewrite the state diagram to begin at S1. Update the entry point: "Entry: Triggered by meaningful exposure to AI (demonstration, workplace mandate, peer adoption event, media narrative, personal experience)."

2. **Reorganize the Limits of operationalization section** to add a new subsection: **"Untested but Falsifiable Hypotheses"** containing:
   - S3B/S7A heuristic distinction (requires longitudinal observation across model releases; contaminated by self-report after reading)
   - S5 dual-component stability claim (no measurement protocol; currently untested)
   - S7F S3-prerequisite claim (refutable by documenting stable S7F without prior S3)
   - Frequency claim: proportion of users who cycle through the model multiple times (no prevalence data)
   - Cultural variability (core structure invariant, intensity/flavor shifts by culture; propose test across five cultural contexts)

3. **Rewrite the S3B Bargaining section:** Remove the S3B/S7A heuristic from main text. Flag it: "See Limits of operationalization for testability status."

4. **Rewrite S5:** Downgrade dual-component claim. Suggested language: "Stability appears to involve both an internal mental-model shift and social normalization, but neither is operationalized and the claim is untested."

5. **Rewrite S7F:** Remove the S3-prerequisite claim from main text. Suggested language: "Most observed S7F users have passed through S3 (their lived experience makes them credible guides), but this is untested."

6. **Add a minor note to D1–D4:** "These are qualitatively distinct buckets, not sharply bounded categories. Practitioners should treat them as a rough scale."

7. **Delete the Cultural Variability section** from main model. Move to Limits as: "**Future research: cross-cultural validation.** Preliminary hypothesis: core state structure is culturally invariant; intensity and emotional flavor shift by culture. Proposed test: measure state sequence and dwell time across high craft-identity, techno-optimistic, collectivist, hierarchical, and economic-anxiety cultures. A finding that would falsify the model: the existence of a culture where stable S7A users regularly enter directly from S0 without S1."
