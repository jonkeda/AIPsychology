# One-Shot Improvement — Model V5 → V6

## TL;DR

- **Consolidate the four S6 overuse states (S6A, S6B, S6C, S6D) into a single S6 state, and add explicit transition markers for S6→S7A.**
- The four S6 variants collapse to one root mechanism (resource depletion); the distinction obscures what matters.
- V5 leaves practitioners blind at the highest-risk point: they see someone burning out but the model offers no framework for intervention.

## The Single Change

**Collapse S6A, S6B, S6C, S6D into a single "S6 — Unsustainable Engagement" state, unified under resource depletion as the sole mechanism. Add a sub-section specifying three observable markers that distinguish "someone about to stabilize to S7A" from "someone headed to S7D (Burnout)".**

Here is the concrete reorganization:

1. **Rename** the four current S6 states to "S6 variants" or remove them entirely.
2. **Describe S6** as: *"I'm using AI at an unsustainable intensity. The driver varies — enthusiasm, anxiety, productivity pressure, peer conformity — but the outcome is the same: cognitive load and regulatory resource depletion."*
3. **Add a single mechanism anchor:** resource depletion (the finite budget of attention and decision-making capacity gets exhausted).
4. **Add a new subsection: "S6 Exit Markers — When S6 Becomes S7A vs. S7D"** with three observable behavioral signals that predict which end state someone lands in.

**Why this beats every other change I considered.**

First, the current four S6 states pretend there are four different problems when there is actually one. S6A person uses AI compulsively because dopamine reward structures. S6B uses it anxiously because self-efficacy collapsed. S6C uses it relentlessly because achievement goals override sustainable pace. S6D uses it because peers are. Four different *drivers*, but one identical *consequence*: unsustainable intensity that depletes a finite resource (attention, self-trust, energy).

V5 admits this implicitly: all four transitions converge at S7D (Burnout). The model is saying "these four roads all lead to burnout." If all roads lead to the same place, why are there four roads? The subdivision creates false precision. A practitioner trying to identify where someone is will look at surface behavior (usage volume, style of complaints) and guess wrongly between S6A and S6B. That matters operationally — because the wrong guess leads to the wrong intervention.

Second, and more important: **V5 provides almost no guidance on exiting S6 into stability (S7A) rather than into burnout.** This is the highest-leverage point in the entire model. Someone in S6 is actively misusing a tool; someone in S5 or S7A has integrated it healthily. The gap between those two states is where organizations and individuals can intervene. But V5 just says S5 transitions to S6 states, and then those states either collapse or exit cleanly. The mechanism of "exit cleanly" is blank.

Consolidating S6 and then adding explicit exit markers would answer: *What does someone in S6 do that tells you they're self-correcting toward maturity rather than spiraling toward burnout?* The current model has no answer. Adding three markers — behavioral observables like "rejects new use cases without testing them" or "rebuilds independent judgment on familiar tasks" or "reports that pausing AI use no longer feels threatening" — would make the model prescriptive where it's currently only descriptive.

Third, consolidation reduces cognitive load on practitioners. If you're training a team on this model, you spend five minutes explaining what S6A is, what S6B is, what S6C is, what S6D is, and why they're different. With one S6 anchored to one mechanism, you spend one minute. That time is redeployed to understanding the exit criteria that actually matter.

## The Four Runners-Up

1. **Develop the Social Context layer from three bullets into a full sub-model with explicit mechanisms.** Currently it's listed as "social contagion," "cultural framing," "normalization" with no causal chains. Given that S5 requires social normalization as a stability component, and given that the surrounding field shapes transition rates, this layer is undersized relative to its importance.

2. **Add a dropout feedback loop: when multiple people drop out for similar reasons, what organizational responses prevent the next cohort from dropping?** The current model treats dropout as an individual exit. But in organizational settings, dropouts cluster by reason and context. The model should explain how to learn from dropout patterns to change the field conditions that caused them.

3. **Specify the S3B exit criteria with measured signals — when does bargaining become understanding vs. when does it collapse back to Ego Shock?** The current heuristic (boundaries move under capability pressure in S3B, only on measured evidence in S7A) requires longitudinal observation across model releases. That's too high a bar for practitioners. Observable behavioral markers (e.g., "person runs the same benchmark test twice" or "person explicitly documents the scope boundary") would operationalize this.

4. **Replace the "Limits of operationalization" section with a practical measurement guide — for each snapshot-observable and longitudinal-observable claim, specify what to measure or ask.** The current section is honest about what's untestable. But honesty without actionability is incomplete. Practitioners need to know: if I want to place someone in S3B vs. S7A, what specifically do I observe or ask?

## What I Considered And Rejected

- **Add a new "S6E — Denial" state** for people who insist they're fine while exhibiting overuse symptoms. Rejected: this is not a separate attractor state; it's a sub-narrative within the four existing S6 variants. Splitting further adds noise without new dynamics.

- **Introduce a numerical "intensity slider" to replace the lost V4 formula.** Rejected: V5 is right that multiplying undefined units is nonsense. Numbers would feel scientific without being rigorous. Behavioral markers are more honest.

- **Rename S7A, S7B, S7C, S7F to remove the hierarchical-sounding labels** (since they're not phases). Rejected: the labels are already addressed as "end states" and their parallel status is clear. Renaming is cosmetic; the consolidation of S6 is structural.

- **Move S7D (Burnout) back into S6 as the natural exit for all overuse.** Rejected: V5 already signals that S7D is reachable from multiple S6 variants. The distinction that matters is that S7D is a *state of withdrawal*, not a state of engagement. It deserves its own section. The issue is not location; it's the missing S6→S7A map.
