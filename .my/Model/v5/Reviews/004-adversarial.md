# Adversarial / Steel-Man Critique — Model V5

## TL;DR

- **Mechanism anchors add no explanatory power.** They are labels (such as "identity-protection cognition") attached to behaviors the model already described. None of the anchors' predictions have been tested against the model's claims.
- **The "Limits of operationalization" section is a confession, not an advance.** It admits that V4's core distinctions — S2T Trust Evaluation versus S2D Defensive Resistance, S3B Bargaining versus S7A Maturity, the dual-component S5 Understanding — cannot be measured. These weren't newly discovered limits in V5; they were always there.
- **V5 is V4 with humbler claims and longer footnotes.** State structure, axes, and transitions are identical. The major additions are admissions of unmeasurability and mechanism labels without tested predictions.
- **The dropout two-axis system is dimensionally empty.** A 2D plane with no metric, no empirical scoring procedure, and an "operational test" that is circular: if the person won't use a "better tool," conclude it was emotional severity — but that just means you failed to make a tool they wanted to use.
- **Identity-protection cognition is too broad to be falsifying.** The model anchors S2D Defensive Resistance, S3 Ego Shock, S3B Bargaining, and S7C Ethical Integration to identity-protection mechanisms. Attaching the same mechanism to four states doesn't distinguish them; it just says "people protect their identity in all of them."
- **Regression as a property, not an exception, makes falsification impossible.** The model now says any state can regress to any earlier state. Did a person stay in S2D Defensive Resistance for years? They must not have experienced a trigger. Did they leave immediately? Trigger happened. The framework explains every outcome after the fact.
- V5 is intellectually more honest than V4. That is not the same as making a better model. The core problem persists: V5 is a taxonomy of observed behaviors dressed in mechanism-anchor language, with no mechanism that constrains which behaviors are possible.

## Findings

### 1. Mechanism anchors are descriptive relabeling, not explanatory anchoring

The model anchors S2D Defensive Resistance to "identity-protection cognition" (citing Kahan and Festinger). That is the label for the already-described behavior: asymmetric updating, discounting positive evidence, amplifying negative evidence. Adding the name of a psychological mechanism does not explain why that behavior happens or predict what the person will do next.

An anchor would have explanatory power if it narrowed the space of possible behaviors. For example, if "identity-protection cognition" predicted that S2D Defensive Resistance people would accept any evidence framed as protecting their identity (even if it contradicts earlier positions), the anchor would be falsifying. The model doesn't make that prediction. S2D Defensive Resistance is still just "asymmetric updating." The mechanism label is **decoration**.

Not a single mechanism anchor has a tested prediction. No section says "if S2D Defensive Resistance is driven by identity protection, then we should observe X behavior that differs from S2T Trust Evaluation in way Y." The anchors are **one-way labels**, not two-way constraints.

### 2. "Limits of operationalization" admits the model was never falsifiable

The new section lists what cannot be measured: S2T Trust Evaluation versus S2D Defensive Resistance (both look like testing, but only S2D Defensive Resistance shows asymmetric updating "only visible across multiple evaluations"); S3B Bargaining versus S7A Maturity (the heuristic requires longitudinal observation across model releases); the dual-component S5 Understanding (internal reframing and social normalization both unobservable).

These are not new limits. V4 claimed S2T Trust Evaluation and S2D Defensive Resistance were distinguishable states; V5 admits the only way to distinguish them is to watch someone's behavior change across multiple model releases. That is not a measurement; that is a narrative applied after observing the choice. You observe someone's choices, decide whether the updates were "asymmetric," and retroactively assign the state.

If the only way to distinguish two states is to watch outcomes across time and judge whether behavior matches the state's definition, the states are not separate claims about reality; they are **interpretive frames** applied after the fact.

### 3. The dual-component S5 Understanding is post-hoc unfalsifiable

S5 Understanding requires both "internal reframing" and "social normalization." Neither is measurable. The model itself notes: "the dual-component requirement is currently conjecture. No instrument is specified for either component, and any observed discomfort can be attributed to mismatch."

This is the classic move: define a success state with two independent unmeasurable components. Outcome follows the model? Person had internal reframing. Outcome contradicts it? Lacked social normalization. The model survives every outcome. A person who rejects AI after reaching S5 Understanding is explained as "lacking social normalization," not as model failure.

### 4. The dropout two-axis plane is mathematically hollow

V5 replaces V4's binary dropout split with a 2D plane (Practical severity 0–10, Emotional severity 0–10). But the model provides no definition of how to score a position, no empirical data on dropout distributions, and no prediction of what region of the plane predicts what recovery outcome.

The "operational test": provide a better tool; if they restart, practical severity was high; if not, emotional was. This is **circular**. If someone doesn't restart after you provide what you think is a better tool, you conclude the problem was emotional. But the only evidence that it was "better" is that it's better according to your judgment, not theirs. You have not measured anything; you have assigned a label after the fact.

### 5. Identity-protection cognition is universal, therefore not distinguishing

The model anchors multiple states to identity-protection: S2D Defensive Resistance ("identity-protection cognition"), S3 Ego Shock ("self-efficacy collapse"), S3B Bargaining ("cognitive dissonance reduction"), S7C Ethical Integration ("meaning-making"). Kahan's work shows motivated reasoning is a robust cross-domain phenomenon.

If identity protection explains four different states, it does not distinguish them. All four show identity-protective behavior. Why is the S2D Defensive Resistance person's asymmetric updating a different state from the S3 Ego Shock person's existential discomfort? The model's implicit answer is: intensity differs. But intensity is not measured and not predicted by the mechanism; it is modulated by the two axes (Identity Stakes × Task Delegation), which are orthogonal to the anchor. A mechanism anchor that applies to four different states is not constraining the model; it is naming a **universal behavior** and calling it an explanation.

### 6. The S7F Evangelism-requires-S3 Ego Shock claim is flagged as untested but constrains the model anyway

The model speculates: "Most stable when the person has previously passed through S3 Ego Shock. Lived experience of Ego Shock is what makes them credible to others currently in it; without that experience, the advocacy tends to be naive (S6A Enthusiastic Overuse flavor) or compelled (S6D Social Overuse flavor) and collapses under the first hostile question." Then immediately: "the 'S3 Ego Shock prerequisite for stable S7F Evangelism' claim is currently conjecture."

If conjecture, it should not shape model structure. If true, it should be a structural requirement. V5 presents it as both: a constraint on what "stable S7F Evangelism" means and a conjecture. This lets the model absorb counterexamples — a successful S7F Evangelism evangelist who never passed through S3 Ego Shock would simply be "unstable S7F Evangelism" or "S6D Social Overuse flavor," not a refutation.

### 7. The S3B Bargaining/S7A Maturity heuristic is retroactively applied

The model offers a heuristic to distinguish S3B Bargaining from S7A Maturity: "In S3B Bargaining, boundaries are renegotiated under capability pressure. In S7A Maturity, boundaries move only after measured evidence."

But whether a person is in S3B Bargaining or S7A Maturity determines how you interpret the same behavior. Suppose a person has bounded adoption and a new model is released. They don't change their boundary. In S3B Bargaining you interpret this as "awaiting the next threat that will shatter the boundary." In S7A Maturity you interpret this as "tested and the evidence wasn't compelling." The same non-action means opposite things depending on which state you assigned them to first. A distinction that is contaminated by the subject's self-report and requires longitudinal observation to apply is not a property of the states; it is a **post-hoc narrative**.

### 8. Mechanism anchors do not constrain the model's transitions

A real structural constraint would say: "If a person is in S2D Defensive Resistance, then they cannot directly transition to S5 Understanding without passing through S3 Ego Shock."

The model makes no such prediction. S2D Defensive Resistance can transition to S5 Understanding, S2T Trust Evaluation, S3 Ego Shock, or dropout. Knowing the mechanism anchor tells you nothing about which transition happens. The anchor is a label for the current state, not a predictor of the next state.

### 9. Regression as a property removes falsifiability of the state structure

The model now claims: "Regression is a property of every transition, not a destination. Any state in S5 Understanding–S7 can be pushed back to earlier states by [list of triggers]."

If any state can transition to any earlier state, the core state structure becomes untestable. Person stayed in S2D Defensive Resistance for two years and left abruptly? Trigger happened at year two. Person jumped from S0 Baseline to S5 Understanding without passing through intermediate states? "Possible for low stakes with prior conceptual familiarity." The model has provided an exit for every observed path. A model in which any path is possible is not wrong; it is **empty**.

### 10. V5 downgrades falsifiability claims from V4 without reconceptualizing

V5 says: "Several V4 claims are downgraded from 'falsifiable' to 'heuristic' where the model lacks a workable measurement protocol."

Presented as a refinement; in fact, an admission that V4's falsifiability claims were aspirational. The solution to "this claim is not falsifiable" is not "flag it as a heuristic and keep it." It is "remove it or develop a measurement protocol." The model inherits its entire state structure from V4. Added mechanism labels don't constrain. Admitted measurement limits were always there. The 2D dropout plane is unmeasurable. None of these changes address the underlying issue: the model has **no testable predictions**.

## Recommendations

1. **Run an empirical study before V6.** Pick two state transitions (S1 Initial Encounter → S2T Trust Evaluation versus S1 Initial Encounter → S2D Defensive Resistance; S2D Defensive Resistance → S3 Ego Shock versus S2D Defensive Resistance → S3B Bargaining) and gather behavioral data on the actual signals. Do subjects in S2T Trust Evaluation show symmetric updating and subjects in S2D Defensive Resistance show asymmetric updating? Can you measure it? Can you predict which transition a person will take? Run this study. Publish results. If the transitions are real, V6 can anchor to tested mechanisms. If not, V6 needs to restructure the states.

2. **Choose between testable and descriptive.** Either develop testable predictions (by running recommendation 1) or admit the model is descriptive-only (a thinking tool and shared vocabulary, not a causal model). Staying in the middle is the worst position. It is neither testable nor transparent.

3. **If you keep the model as-is, fix the anchor language.** Change "Mechanism anchor:" to "Related literature:" This signals the connection is conceptual, not explanatory. Stop claiming the mechanism constrains the model.

4. **Stop adding detail before measuring the skeleton.** V4 → V5 added mechanism anchors (unmeasured), a 2D dropout plane (unmeasured), and a Limits section (admitting unmeasurability). V5 → V6 should not add more states, more axes, or more mechanism labels. Test the existing structure or simplify it.
