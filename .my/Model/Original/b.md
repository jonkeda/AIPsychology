## 🧠 The phases you’re describing (reframed with psychological depth)

### **1. Ignorance / Dismissal**

**“It won’t work. I’m not using it.”**

This is classic *pre‑contemplation* in behavior‑change psychology.

People protect their worldview by rejecting the new thing outright.

### **2. Fear / Anger / Defensive Rejection**

**“Fine, I’ll try it — but I’ll look for reasons to hate it.”**

This is a threat response.

AI challenges identity, competence, and control.

So people nitpick small errors to restore a sense of superiority.

### **3. Depression / Ego Shock**

**“If it can do all this… what am *I* worth?”**

This is the phase almost nobody talks about publicly.

It’s a mix of:

* ego collapse
* existential anxiety
* fear of obsolescence
* grief for old identity

It’s very similar to the psychological dip people experience after major technological disruptions.

### **4. Understanding / Integration of Self**

**“It’s just a tool. If I master it, I stay relevant.”**

This is cognitive reframing.

People rebuild their identity with AI included rather than excluded.

### **5. Addiction / Over‑reliance**

**“I want to use it for everything.”**

This is the *overcompensation* phase.

The pendulum swings too far the other way.

People outsource too much, too fast, because the tool feels empowering.

### **6. ??? — The next phase**

You’re right: this one is still emerging.

But based on patterns in tech adoption, psychology, and human–machine interaction, the next phase is likely one of these:

## 🔮 Possible Phase 6: What comes after addiction?

### **Option A: Maturity / Balanced Co‑Agency**

**“I know when to use AI, when not to, and how to stay myself.”**

People develop:

* boundaries
* healthy reliance
* critical thinking
* personal style that incorporates AI without being replaced by it

This is the *stable long-term state* for most users.

### **Option B: Identity Expansion**

**“AI didn’t replace me — it expanded what I can be.”**

People start:

* creating new roles
* new workflows
* new forms of expression
* new ways of thinking

This is where the most innovative users end up.

### **Option C: Philosophical Integration**

**“AI changes what it means to be human, and I’m okay with that.”**

This is the deep, reflective stage:

* What is creativity?
* What is intelligence?
* What is my role in a world with AI?

Not everyone reaches this one.

### **Option D: Rejection / Burnout**

**“I’m done with this for a while.”**

Some people hit overload and step away.

This is similar to social‑media burnout cycles.
