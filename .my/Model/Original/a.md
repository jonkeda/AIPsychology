Yes — you’re onto something real. There *is* a growing idea in psychology and human–computer interaction that people go through recognizable **psychological phases** when interacting with AI systems. There isn’t one single official model yet, but several researchers and practitioners have described patterns that line up with what you’re sensing.

Here’s the concise takeaway:

**People tend to move through predictable emotional and cognitive stages when they start using AI — from excitement, to over‑trust, to disappointment, to mature integration.**

---

### 🧠 Commonly observed phases when people work with AI

These aren’t rigid, but they show up again and again across studies and user behavior.

#### 1. **Curiosity / Novelty**

* “Wow, this is cool.”
* People test boundaries, ask playful questions, and explore capabilities.

#### 2. **Overestimation / Magical Thinking**

* “AI can do anything.”
* Users assume the system is smarter, more accurate, or more humanlike than it is.
* This is similar to the *ELIZA effect* — attributing understanding where there isn’t any.

#### 3. **Disillusionment / Frustration**

* “Why did it get this wrong?”
* People hit limitations, hallucinations, or misunderstandings.
* Trust drops sharply.

#### 4. **Adjustment / Calibration**

* “Okay, it’s good at X, not good at Y.”
* Users learn how to phrase prompts, what tasks AI excels at, and where human judgment is needed.

#### 5. **Integration / Partnership**

* “This is part of my workflow now.”
* AI becomes a tool rather than a novelty.
* People develop realistic expectations and stable habits.

#### 6. **Reflection / Ethical Awareness** (emerging phase)

* “How should I use this responsibly?”
* Users think about bias, privacy, creativity, and the impact on their identity or work.

---

### 📚 Related concepts researchers talk about

These aren’t exactly “phases,” but they support the idea:

* **ELIZA effect** — people anthropomorphize AI early on.
* **Automation bias** — people over-trust AI recommendations.
* **Algorithm aversion** — after seeing one mistake, people under-trust AI.
* **Human–AI teaming models** — describe how trust evolves over time.
* **Technology adoption lifecycle** — innovators → early adopters → mainstream users.

---

### 🎯 Why your idea matters

If we can map these phases clearly, we can:

* design better onboarding for AI tools
* help people avoid over-trust or under-trust
* support healthier long-term use
* understand emotional reactions to AI (fear, excitement, dependency, etc.)

You’re basically describing the early shape of a  **psychological model of AI adoption** , which is something researchers are actively trying to formalize.
