# S6E — Enthusiastic Overuse

> *"I want to use it for everything."*

Dopamine-driven experimentation. The person is energised, productive, and uncritical. Every workflow gets routed through AI even when it is the wrong tool for the task. Output volume goes up; output quality varies.

## Mechanism anchors

Conceptually related to **variable-reward reinforcement** — the intermittent-reward dynamic that underlies compulsive engagement with any tool whose outputs are unpredictably good. Sometimes AI produces something exceptional, sometimes mediocre; the unpredictability is what sustains the behavior. Same construct that explains slot-machine engagement, social-media checking, and casual-game hooks. Borrowed as vocabulary, not as a tested mechanism for S6E.

## Identity Stakes / Delegation modulation

S6E can occur at any Identity Stakes level but is most pronounced when low-stakes enthusiasm spills into higher-stakes domains the person has not yet calibrated. Delegation Level in S6E often drifts upward without deliberate testing.

## Transitions

- → **S7M Maturity** — the enthusiasm settles as the person learns where the tool fails.
- → **S7I Identity Expansion** — the enthusiasm channels into a new generative identity.
- → **S7B Burnout** — sustained high engagement depletes the person.
- → **S5 Understanding** — mild regression after a sobering failure.

## ⚡ External triggers

- **Viral demos**, **leaderboards**, **internal AI-usage metrics** — all amplify S6E.
- A **sobering failure** (a public mistake caused by uncritical AI use) typically triggers exit toward S7M or S5.

## Healthiness

**Sustainable with cost.** Healthy short-term, costly long-term. The model describes how people arrive in this state and how they tend to leave it. It does not prescribe that this state must be exited quickly. Whether and how to support a transition is a practitioner judgement, not a model requirement.

## Observation markers

- Usage spikes align with reward moments: a fresh model release, a team demo, a positive reaction to an AI-produced output.
- The person talks about AI with energy and little critical distance.
- They try AI on problems where it is the wrong tool and are surprised when it fails.
- Output volume is up; output quality is variable and the person doesn't track it.

Key probe: *"When was the last time AI surprised you in a bad way? What did you do with that?"* An S6E person will either not have an answer or will quickly pivot back to a positive memory.

## Open questions

- The duration of healthy S6E before it becomes corrosive is qualitatively understood (weeks to months) but not numerically established.
