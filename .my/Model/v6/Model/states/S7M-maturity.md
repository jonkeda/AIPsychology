# S7M — Maturity

> *"I know when to use AI and when not to."*

Bounded adoption with calibrated scope. The person has a clear internal model of where AI helps and where it does not, and routes work accordingly. Behaviorally similar to [S3B Bargaining](S3B-bargaining.md), distinguished by where the boundary sits and how it moves.

## Mechanism anchors

Conceptually related to **metacognitive calibration** — accurate self-assessment of when one's own judgement is better than the tool's. The mature user has an internal model of the tool's failure modes (where it hallucinates, where it sycophants, where it confidently averages out the interesting answer) and routes work to avoid those modes. Adjacent literature: research on calibration of confidence with accuracy (Lichtenstein and Fischhoff, and the modern decision-science extensions). Borrowed as vocabulary.

## Identity Stakes / Delegation modulation

S7M is reachable in any Identity Stakes context. In high-stakes domains S7M typically follows S3E Ego Shock and S3B Bargaining; in low-stakes domains it can be reached more directly. Delegation Level varies by task — the S7M user delegates at high D-levels in their core domain when AI is reliable enough there, and refuses to delegate at low D-levels outside their core domain when AI is not reliable enough there.

## Transitions

- → **S3E Ego Shock** — regression when a capability jump invalidates the calibration.
- → **S7I Identity Expansion**, **S7E Ethical Integration**, **S7V Evangelism** — lateral shift; S7M often co-occurs with these rather than transitioning out.
- → **Practical-flavoured Dropout** — even mature users leave when the tool degrades.

## ⚡ External triggers

⚡ Re-triggered by: **model releases** that move capability past the person's calibrated boundaries.

## Healthiness

**Sustainable.** S7M is the model's cleanest sustainable state. Note the assumption built into this state: it requires viable AI-collaborative work to remain in the person's domain. S7M is not available to someone whose economic position has been eliminated by AI. See [S3X Structural Displacement](S3X-structural-displacement.md).

## Heuristic distinction from S3B Bargaining

(See [S3B Bargaining](S3B-bargaining.md) for the full discussion.)

S7M differs from S3B on two axes:

- **Where the boundary sits** — competence line, not identity line.
- **How the boundary moves** — on measured evidence, not under capability pressure.

The S7M user asks "Is AI reliable enough at this specific task?" The S3B user asks "Does this task feel too important to delegate?"

S7M behavior in practice: the user delegates at high D-levels in their core domain when AI is reliable enough there, and refuses to delegate at low D-levels outside their core domain when AI is not reliable enough there. The identity engagement does not determine the choice; the quality of AI does.

## Observation markers

- Boundaries map onto AI's reliability profile, not onto the person's professional identity.
- When pressed on a hypothetical model release, the person says "I'd need to test that before changing how I use it."
- The person can describe specific failure modes of the tools they use.

## Open questions

- **Testable —** boundary-movement behavior across multiple model releases distinguishes S7M from S3B. Note: this signal requires longitudinal observation and is contaminated by self-report once a subject has read the model.
