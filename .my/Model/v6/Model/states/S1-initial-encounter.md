# S1 — Initial Encounter

> *"What is this thing?"*

First substantive contact. Stance is unformed. Emotional flavour (curiosity, scepticism, threat) depends on Identity Stakes, not on this state itself.

## Mechanism anchors

Conceptually related to **novelty appraisal** — the brain's first-pass classification of an unfamiliar stimulus as relevant or irrelevant, threatening or benign. Lazarus and Folkman's work on stress appraisal is the closest classical reference: people first decide *what is this and does it matter to me*, then decide *what can I do about it*. The borrowing is conceptual: novelty appraisal is offered as vocabulary for what S1 is, not as a tested mechanism for the state.

## Identity Stakes / Delegation modulation

Identity Stakes determines which fork the person takes out of S1. Low-stakes encounters typically move to [S2T Trust Evaluation](S2T-trust-evaluation.md). High-stakes encounters typically move to [S2D Defensive Resistance](S2D-defensive-resistance.md). The split is a tendency, not a deterministic rule — a person with high stakes who is psychologically secure may enter S2T, and a person with apparently low stakes may discover during testing that the stakes were higher than they realised.

Task Delegation Level is not yet meaningful in S1 — the person has not yet committed to delegation patterns.

## Transitions

- → **S2T Trust Evaluation** — typical for low Identity Stakes.
- → **S2D Defensive Resistance** — typical for high Identity Stakes.
- → **S5 Understanding** — possible for low-stakes encounters with prior conceptual familiarity (the engineer who reads the literature before first hands-on use).
- → **S2P Pre-emptive Non-Adoption** — for users who perform rapid ethical appraisal at first encounter and decline without testing. See [S2P](S2P-pre-emptive-non-adoption.md).

## ⚡ External triggers

- **Workplace mandates** compress S0 → S1 → next-state, often skipping deliberation.
- **Peer adoption events** (a respected colleague's visible adoption) can compress S1.
- **Compelling demos** can move a person from S1 to S2T or S5 within a single session.

## Healthiness

S1 is **transitional only**. Healthy as a passage, harmful as a residence. Long stays in S1 typically indicate the person is unable to form a stance — usually for structural reasons (no real opportunity to engage) rather than psychological ones.

## Observation markers

- The person can describe one or two specific AI interactions but has not yet committed to a usage pattern.
- They ask basic orientation questions ("what is it good at?", "is it accurate?") without a defended position.
- They do not yet have a routine that includes or excludes AI.

## Open questions

- How short S1 can legitimately be (some users move S0 → S1 → S2T inside a single afternoon) is undefined.
- Whether S1 has a meaningful internal duration or is better modelled as a transition than a state remains a design choice.
