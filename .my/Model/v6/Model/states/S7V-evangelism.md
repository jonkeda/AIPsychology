# S7V — Influence / Evangelism

> *"Let me show you what this can do."*

The person's identity becomes partly defined by their role as a guide for others' adoption: teaching, mentoring, internal advocacy, public writing, conference speaking, community-building. Distinct from [S7M Maturity](S7M-maturity.md) and [S7I Identity Expansion](S7I-identity-expansion.md) in that the locus of meaning is *external* — helping others through the cycle is what makes the role coherent for the person.

## Mechanism anchors

Conceptually related to **generativity** (Erikson) — broadened from its original developmental-stage context, the drive to invest in the next cohort's success. The same vocabulary that explains why senior practitioners across many domains turn to mentoring and teaching once their own competence is settled. AI provides an unusually sharp opportunity for this because the cohort needing guidance is large and the experience required to guide them is a few years rather than a few decades. Borrowed as vocabulary.

## Identity Stakes / Delegation modulation

S7V is high-effort regardless of Stakes or Delegation. The person's own use is whatever it would otherwise be; what defines S7V is the externally-directed advocacy work layered on top.

## Origins of stable S7V

People reach stable S7V through multiple paths:

- **Through S3E Ego Shock.** Lived experience of identity disruption is one common origin. People who have been through Ego Shock often become credible guides for others currently in it.
- **Through S7I Identity Expansion.** People who have built a new generative identity around AI sometimes channel that into helping others build theirs.
- **Through professional role.** Educators, consultants, and team leads can occupy stable S7V because the role demands it, with or without prior S3E experience.
- **Through generativity drive.** Some people are oriented toward helping others develop in any domain they themselves have worked through, and AI is just the latest instance.

The S3E path is *one* origin, not the only origin. V5 implied otherwise; V6 downgrades the claim: stable S7V is *more common* among people with prior S3E experience, but stable S7V without prior S3E also exists.

A claim V6 still makes: S7V that is **only** [S6E Enthusiastic Overuse](S6E-enthusiastic-overuse.md) flavoured (uncritical promotion without depth of prior engagement) tends to collapse under hostile questioning. This is about depth of engagement, not specifically about S3E.

## Transitions

- → **S7B Burnout** — evangelism is high-effort; sustained advocacy depletes.
- → **S6E** or **S6S** — collapse when depth of engagement is missing.
- → **S7M Maturity** — the external role recedes back into private mature practice.

## ⚡ External triggers

⚡ Re-triggered by: **external demand for guidance** (organisational AI rollouts, public discourse shifts, requests for talks).

## Healthiness

**Sustainable with cost.** The external orientation — helping others through what one has navigated — draws on generativity, which is self-sustaining for many people. But sustained advocacy is high-effort. The path to S7B Burnout is real from S7V, especially when external demand exceeds personal capacity or when the audience is hostile. Watch for energy decline and rising cynicism.

Note the same assumption as S7M and S7I: S7V assumes viable AI-collaborative work exists in the person's domain. Evangelism for an integration that is not actually available to the audience can degrade into something else over time.

## Observation markers

- The person has visible external advocacy work (talks, articles, mentoring relationships).
- They can speak from depth — examples of failure as well as success — under hostile questioning.
- Their advocacy survives shifts in audience or platform.

## Open questions

- **Testable —** the claim that S7V without depth of engagement collapses under hostile questioning is observable across multiple speaking events. This is a softer claim than V5's "S7V requires prior S3E."
