# S6D — Dependent Overuse

> *"I don't trust my own abilities anymore."*

The person uses AI as a crutch because [S3E Ego Shock](S3E-ego-shock.md) eroded their confidence in their own judgement. Surface behavior looks like [S6E Enthusiastic Overuse](S6E-enthusiastic-overuse.md) (high usage volume) but the affect is different — anxiety rather than enthusiasm. The person checks AI output against AI rather than against their own thinking.

## Mechanism anchors

Conceptually related to **self-efficacy displacement** — confidence in the AI replaces, rather than supplements, confidence in self. A reverse application of Bandura's construct: instead of mastery experiences building self-efficacy, repeated outsourcing erodes it. Each successful AI completion is silent evidence that the person's contribution wasn't necessary. Borrowed as vocabulary.

## Identity Stakes / Delegation modulation

S6D is most damaging in **high Identity Stakes** domains, where the eroding self-trust touches core self-concept. Delegation Level in S6D is often higher than the person's prior baseline — they delegate tasks they previously handled alone.

## Transitions

- → **S3E Ego Shock** — regression when the person realises how much they have outsourced.
- → **S7B Burnout** — dependence becomes exhausting to maintain.
- → **S7M Maturity** — rare but possible when the person rebuilds independent judgement alongside AI use.

## ⚡ External triggers

No strong ⚡ marker. S6D is internally driven and inherits its trigger from the upstream S3E episode rather than from any current event.

## Healthiness

**Sustainable but corrosive.** S6D is corrosive when sustained. The mechanism is self-reinforcing: each AI output that succeeds silently confirms the person's own contribution was unnecessary, which further reduces self-trust. Early signals — the person stops attempting tasks without AI involvement, declines to review AI output against their own judgement — are worth responding to before the erosion is substantial.

The model describes how people arrive in this state and how they tend to leave it. It does not prescribe that S6D must be exited quickly.

## Observation markers

- High AI use but anxious affect, not enthusiastic.
- The person checks AI output against other AI output rather than against their own judgement.
- Self-reported confidence in their own work has declined since adoption.
- They struggle to make decisions without AI involvement, even for tasks they handled comfortably before.

Key probe: *"If AI were unavailable for a week, what would you do differently?"* An S6D person will express more anxiety about this than the work difficulty actually warrants.

## Open questions

- **Testable —** the trajectory of S6D into S7B Burnout versus into S7M Maturity is observable across multiple months.
