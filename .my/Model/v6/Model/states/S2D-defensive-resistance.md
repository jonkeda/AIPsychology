# S2D — Defensive Resistance

> *"Fine, I'll try it — but I'm going to find flaws."*

Ego defense. Nitpicking, downplaying strengths, highlighting weaknesses. Identity protection, not objective evaluation. The behavior can look identical to [S2T Trust Evaluation](S2T-trust-evaluation.md) from outside; the difference is **asymmetric updating** — S2D discounts positive evidence and amplifies negative evidence.

Some S2D occupants arrive from S1 directly (the typical high-stakes path); others arrive via S2T (testing surfaced a threat).

## Domain scoping

S2D is **per-domain**, not per-person. The same individual can be S2D in one domain and S2T, S5, or [S7M Maturity](S7M-maturity.md) in another.

The defensive response fires only in domains where the person's identity is on the line. A senior craftsperson can simultaneously be:

- S2D about AI in their craft (high stakes).
- S2T about AI for adjacent professional tasks (moderate stakes).
- S5 about AI for travel planning (low stakes).
- S7M about AI for research summarisation (long-evaluated, low stakes).

When practitioners use this model, they should ask "which domain are we talking about?" before asking "which state are they in?"

## Mechanism anchors

Conceptually related to **identity-protection cognition** (Kahan) — the tendency to evaluate evidence in ways that defend a valued self-concept. Originally Kahan's term in the political-belief literature; the same vocabulary applies to professional identity under capability threat. Borrowed as vocabulary, not as a tested causal account.

Also conceptually related to **cognitive dissonance** (Festinger) — the discomfort of holding two beliefs that contradict each other. The older and broader framing of the same dynamic.

## Identity Stakes / Delegation modulation

S2D requires **high Identity Stakes** in the domain by definition. Without identity at stake, the same evaluation behavior is just S2T. Task Delegation Level intensifies the experience: D3 Creative Delegation or D4 Autonomous Partnership in a high-stakes domain produces the strongest defensive reaction.

## Transitions

- → **S3E Ego Shock** — defenses collapse under capability evidence.
- → **S3B Bargaining** — defenses hold partially; the person negotiates scope.
- → **S1 Initial Encounter** — regression after a poor demonstration.
- → **Practical-flavoured Dropout** — the tool genuinely doesn't fit the workflow.

Inbound: from S1 (the typical high-stakes path), from S2T (when testing reveals an unexpected threat), and as a regression from later states under capability jumps.

## ⚡ External triggers

- **Model release** that overruns the current rebuttal — pushes S2D toward S3E.
- **Visible peer adoption** in the same domain — undermines the social plausibility of the defensive position.

## Healthiness

**Transitional only.** Sustainable but corrosive when held long-term — the person is fighting their own capacity to update. Long-residence S2D in identity-threatened domains is a signal that the org's framing or the person's situation is creating unnecessary identity threat.

The model describes how people arrive in this state and how they tend to leave it. It does not prescribe that S2D must be exited quickly. Whether and how to support a transition is a practitioner judgement, not a model requirement.

## Observation markers

- The person focuses on AI failures and discounts successes.
- When AI succeeds, they attribute it to their prompting skill or to a low-stakes task.
- Their stated view of AI has not moved despite significant testing.
- The defensive stance is restricted to one or two domains; in other domains the same person evaluates AI normally.

The cleanest probe (see [observation-guide.md](../observation-guide.md)): "Tell me about a time AI did something that made you reconsider your view." An S2D person will either not have an answer or will describe something that confirmed their negative view.

## Diagnostic difficulty: S2D versus S2P with ethical language

S2D dressed in ethical language and [S2P Pre-emptive Non-Adoption](S2P-pre-emptive-non-adoption.md) look almost identical from outside. Both decline to engage, both cite principled reasons, both discount capability evidence. See [S2P](S2P-pre-emptive-non-adoption.md) for the diagnostic frame. Most real cases are mixed.

## Open questions

- **Testable —** the asymmetric-updating signal that distinguishes S2T from S2D requires multiple evaluations across model releases.
- Whether long-residence S2D is psychologically distinct from sustained [S3B Bargaining](S3B-bargaining.md) at the limit, or whether they merge in practice, is undefined.
