# S7E — Ethical / Philosophical Integration

> *"What does AI mean for society, creativity, or humanity?"*

Sustained reflection on bias, intellectual property, authenticity, labour displacement, environmental cost, societal effects. The person remains a user — this is **not** a refusal state — but the use is accompanied by ongoing ethical work, deciding what to use AI for and what to keep human, often making those decisions visible to others.

S7E is rarely a sole end state. Most often it co-occurs with [S7M Maturity](S7M-maturity.md) (mature use plus reflective stance) or [S7I Identity Expansion](S7I-identity-expansion.md) (expansion plus reckoning with what was expanded). When it appears alone, the person typically uses AI minimally and treats engagement with the technology as primarily a thinking subject.

## Distinction from S2P Pre-emptive Non-Adoption

[S2P](S2P-pre-emptive-non-adoption.md) is values-grounded *non-use* reached at first encounter, before substantive testing. S7E is ethical reflection that grows *out of* substantive use. Both are values-driven; they are not the same state.

## Mechanism anchors

Conceptually related to **meaning-making** — the broader cognitive process of integrating a personally significant experience into a coherent worldview. Adjacent literature: post-traumatic growth research, which documents that confrontation with destabilising experience can yield a more articulated worldview rather than a return to the prior state. The same vocabulary applies in non-traumatic contexts when an experience is large enough to require reorganisation of existing beliefs. Borrowed as vocabulary.

## Identity Stakes / Delegation modulation

S7E often appears in users with **high Identity Stakes** in domains adjacent to AI's effects (creative work, knowledge work, education). Delegation Level is varied; the choices about delegation are themselves part of the ethical work.

## Transitions

- → **S7M Maturity** — the reflective stance settles into stable practice.
- → **S7V Evangelism** — the reflection becomes externally directed.
- → **S2D Defensive Resistance** — rare; the reflection concludes that prior adoption was a mistake and the person retrenches.

## ⚡ External triggers

No strong ⚡ marker. S7E is internally driven. Triggers exist (a public AI ethics incident, a personal moral dilemma) but they amplify rather than create the state.

## Healthiness

**Sustainable.** Reflection is built into the state. Compatible with stable life and work patterns.

## Observation markers

- The person can articulate specific ethical positions on AI use, with reasons.
- Their actual usage choices align with their stated positions.
- They engage with counter-arguments rather than dismissing them.

## Open questions

- The line between healthy S7E and rumination is qualitatively understood but not operationally specified.
