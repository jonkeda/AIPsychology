# S6S — Social Overuse

> *"Everyone else is using it; I need to keep up."*

Peer-pressure-driven adoption that exceeds the person's actual need or interest. Distinct from [S6E Enthusiastic Overuse](S6E-enthusiastic-overuse.md) (internally enthusiastic) and [S6R Driven Overuse](S6R-driven-overuse.md) (internally driven by productivity goals). The person here is following a perceived norm, not a personal goal.

## Mechanism anchors

Conceptually related to **normative social influence** — behavior change driven by the desire to match peer behavior, independent of personal evaluation of the behavior's merit. Asch's classic conformity work is the canonical reference; the modern variant is the documented tendency of professionals to adopt tools their peers visibly use even when their own work doesn't require them. Borrowed as vocabulary.

## Identity Stakes / Delegation modulation

S6S is largely independent of Identity Stakes — peer pressure operates regardless of whether the domain is identity-central. Delegation Level tracks peer norms rather than personal calibration.

## Transitions

- → **S5 Understanding** — the typical exit when peer pressure fades or the person changes context.
- → **S7B Burnout** — if the pressure persists past the person's capacity.
- → **S6E Enthusiastic Overuse** — occasional drift when external pressure converts into internal enthusiasm.

## ⚡ External triggers

Triggered by: **peer-group adoption shifts**, **leadership announcements**, **industry-wide adoption events**.

## Healthiness

**Sustainable with cost** as long as external pressure does not exceed personal capacity. The model describes how people arrive in this state and how they tend to leave it. It does not prescribe that S6S must be exited quickly.

## Observation markers

- Adoption closely tracks team or peer adoption. When the team goes heavy on AI, so do they; when a respected colleague backs off, so do they.
- They cannot articulate a personal case for their level of AI use — they describe what their team is doing.
- They express discomfort or anxiety about being seen as "behind" rather than about the work itself.

Key probe: *"What would you do with AI if you were working on this entirely alone, with no one watching?"* An S6S person's hypothetical answer will often differ significantly from their observed behavior.

## Open questions

- **Conjecture —** the coexistence of S6S with S6D Dependent Overuse in compliance-heavy environments is observed but not directly tested.
