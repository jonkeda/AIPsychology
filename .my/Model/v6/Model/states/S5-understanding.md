# S5 — Understanding

> *"It's powerful, but it's still a tool. I can work with this."*

The first stable state. The person has a working mental model of what AI is and isn't, knows where human judgement matters, and prompts effectively.

## Mechanism anchors

This state borrows from **Diffusion of Innovations** (Rogers) — adoption depends on the innovation's perceived compatibility with existing practice. S5 is the moment that compatibility is achieved at the individual level. Borrowed conceptually as vocabulary.

Also conceptually related to **schema integration** — the cognitive process by which a new tool is incorporated into existing mental models of work, rather than competing with them.

## Stability has two components

Both required:

- **Internal reframing** — personal mental model is coherent.
- **Social normalisation** — surrounding environment treats AI use as unremarkable.

**Conjecture —** the dual-component requirement is currently a hypothesis. No instrument is specified for either component, and any observed discomfort can be attributed to mismatch. Treated as a hypothesis to test, not a tested claim.

## Identity Stakes / Delegation modulation

S5 is reachable from any Identity Stakes level. For high-stakes domains, S5 is typically reached *after* S3E Ego Shock or S3B Bargaining; for low-stakes domains, S5 is often reached directly from S2T Trust Evaluation. Delegation Level in S5 is varied — the user routes work pragmatically.

## Transitions

- → **S6E Enthusiastic Overuse, S6D Dependent Overuse, S6R Driven Overuse, S6S Social Overuse** — enthusiasm or pressure tips into excess.
- → **S7M Maturity** — direct stabilisation for measured adopters.
- → **S7I Identity Expansion** or **S7E Ethical Integration** — direct stabilisation into other end states.
- → **S3E Ego Shock** — regression via external trigger (capability jump in a previously bounded domain).

A note on transitional shapes: a person who draws their boundary at the identity line (looks like [S3B Bargaining](S3B-bargaining.md)) but is increasingly moving the boundary on evidence is typically transitioning from S3B toward [S7M Maturity](S7M-maturity.md), passing through S5.

## ⚡ External triggers

- **Capability jumps** can push S5 occupants back to S3E.
- **Workplace metric changes** can tip S5 toward S6R or S6S.

## Healthiness

**Sustainable.** S5 is functional and stable; healthy, if less integrated than S7M Maturity. A person who reaches S5 and remains there is not failing.

## Note on assistive-technology users

This model assumes the user's baseline capability exists and AI is matching (replicating) an existing skill. Users who rely on AI as **assistive technology** — for whom AI provides access to tasks they could not otherwise perform — sit outside the model's mechanism layer. For these users, S3E Ego Shock is typically absent, [S7I Identity Expansion](S7I-identity-expansion.md) is common, and S2D Defensive Resistance rarely applies. See [populations.md](../populations.md).

## Observation markers

- The person can articulate where AI helps and where it does not in their work.
- Usage decisions are made case by case, not as a defended stance.
- Routine includes AI without anxiety or compulsion.

## Open questions

- **Conjecture —** the dual-component (internal + social) stability requirement.
- Whether S5 is best modelled as a stable end state in its own right or as a transitional plateau before S7 states is undefined; observation suggests both occur.
