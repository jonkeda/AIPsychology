# S6R — Driven Overuse

> *"I must automate everything."*

Productivity-pressure-driven, not emotion-driven. Common in high-performance work cultures and among individuals with strong achievement orientation. The person uses AI not because they enjoy it ([S6E](S6E-enthusiastic-overuse.md)) or because they doubt themselves ([S6D](S6D-dependent-overuse.md)), but because not using it feels like leaving performance on the table.

## Mechanism anchors

Conceptually related to **goal substitution** in the cognitive-psychology work on goal hierarchies — efficiency, originally a means to other goals (more output, better life balance, career advancement), becomes a terminal goal in itself. The documented tendency of subgoals to displace the superordinate goals they were meant to serve. Borrowed as vocabulary.

## Identity Stakes / Delegation modulation

S6R is largely independent of Identity Stakes — it fires under productivity pressure regardless of whether the domain is identity-central. Delegation Level escalates as the person seeks new efficiencies; D3 Creative Delegation and D4 Autonomous Partnership become routine.

## Transitions

- → **S7B Burnout** — the typical exit. There is no natural ceiling on "more efficient," so the person runs until depleted.
- → **S7M Maturity** — if a structural change (new role, new manager, illness) forces re-evaluation.

## ⚡ External triggers

Triggered or amplified by: **workplace AI-usage metrics**, **productivity-tracking rollouts**, **performance-review changes** that explicitly reward AI use.

## Healthiness

**Sustainable with cost** only as long as external pressure does not exceed personal capacity. Past that point, S7B Burnout follows. The model describes how people arrive in this state and how they tend to leave it. It does not prescribe that S6R must be exited quickly.

## Observation markers

- The person uses AI for everything, but with an efficiency framing, not an excitement framing.
- They describe AI as something they "have to" use to stay competitive or meet targets.
- AI use tracks productivity targets — more use when targets are high or tracked, less when not.
- Goals shift: the AI efficiency goal is crowding out the original work goal.

Key probe: *"If performance metrics stopped tracking AI use tomorrow, how would your usage change?"* An S6R person will often admit it would drop significantly, or reveal that they find the current pace unsustainable.

## Open questions
