# S3E — Ego Shock

> *"If AI can do this… what does that make me?"*

The emotional core of the model. Anxiety, fear of obsolescence, existential discomfort, identity disruption. Qualitative intensity scales with Identity Stakes and Task Delegation Level.

S3E is a **reckoning**, not a grief process. The person is confronting the fact that the skill they built their self-concept around is now also something a tool can do. The work of S3E is reorganising self-concept around that fact, not mourning a lost capability.

## Mechanism anchors

This state borrows from **self-efficacy** (Bandura) — a person's belief that they can actually do the thing. When AI demonstrably performs a task the person built their self-concept around, the belief that "I can do this" no longer carries unique weight. Borrowed conceptually as vocabulary; self-efficacy collapse is offered as a frame for thinking about S3E, not as a tested cause.

A secondary connection is to **terror management** in the loose sense — confrontation with one's own replaceability triggers the same kind of existential discomfort that confrontation with mortality does. The two frames compound; S3E is rarely cleanly one or the other.

## Identity Stakes / Delegation modulation

| Stakes × D-Level | Experienced as |
| --- | --- |
| Low × D1 | Mild surprise — "It's useful" |
| Low × D4 | Curiosity tinged with vertigo |
| High × D2 | Discomfort — "It does this faster than me" |
| High × D3 | Identity disruption — "It can do what I trained for" |
| High × D4 | Existential reckoning — "What is my role now?" |

## Transitions

- → **S3B Bargaining** — negotiate to reduce intensity. The most common path.
- → **S5 Understanding** — direct integration when the person can hold the full confrontation without negotiating scope. **Rare.** Most people who appear to take this path are actually doing accelerated S3B and revisiting it later. **Testable —** distinguishing the direct path from a fast S3E → S3B → S5 path requires longitudinal observation.
- → **S3X Structural Displacement** — when economic non-viability is established. The identity reckoning is joined by a structural one. See [S3X](S3X-structural-displacement.md).
- → **Emotional-flavoured Dropout** — when the psychological cost exceeds what the person is willing to pay.

S3E is also reachable as a regression from every S5–S7 state when capability jumps invalidate the person's current calibration.

## ⚡ External triggers

- **Capability jumps** in the person's domain.
- **A junior colleague outperforming with AI** — re-triggers S3E from later states.
- **A model release** that overruns prior rebuttals.

## Healthiness

S3E is **transitional only**. Healthy as a passage; harmful as a residence. Sustained S3E without movement to S3B Bargaining or S5 Understanding within weeks to months in the same domain warrants serious attention. Identity disruption that does not resolve after six or more months is within the range of clinical distress, not ordinary adaptation discomfort, and may warrant a referral consideration.

The model does not prescribe that S3E must be exited quickly. Whether and how to support a transition is a practitioner judgement, not a model requirement.

## Observation markers

- The person voices identity-level questions, not capability-level questions ("what does this make me?" rather than "what can it do?").
- They withdraw from the domain rather than engaging with the tool.
- Affect is anxious or destabilised; not sad in the bereavement sense.
- A practitioner should look for whether the person is **moving through** S3E (transitioning toward S3B or S5 within weeks to months) or **staying** (identity disruption unresolved after six or more months in the same domain).

## Open questions

- **Testable —** distinguishing direct S3E → S5 from accelerated S3E → S3B → S5 on a single observation is not possible.
- Whether the existential register of S3E differs structurally from grief or is best understood as a separate process remains a definitional question.
