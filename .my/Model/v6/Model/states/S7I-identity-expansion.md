# S7I — Identity Expansion

> *"AI extends what I can be."*

The person develops capability or creative output they couldn't produce alone and partly defines themselves through this expansion. Distinct from [S7M Maturity](S7M-maturity.md): S7M is stewardship of an existing identity; S7I is the construction of a new one. A novelist who uses AI carefully to polish their voice is in S7M; a person who couldn't write fiction before AI and now identifies as a writer-with-AI is in S7I.

## Mechanism anchors

Conceptually related to **the extended self** (Belk) — the documented tendency of humans to incorporate tools into their self-concept. The carpenter and the hammer; the writer and the typewriter; the photographer and the camera. With AI the integration is deeper because the tool participates in cognition rather than just executing it, which is why the identity claim feels different from "I'm good with Photoshop." Adjacent literature: the broader phenomenology-of-technology work on tool-self continuity. Borrowed as vocabulary.

## Identity Stakes / Delegation modulation

S7I tends to involve **high Delegation Levels** (D3 Creative Delegation and D4 Autonomous Partnership) in the expanded domain. Identity Stakes is rebuilt around the expanded capability rather than around a pre-AI baseline.

## Transitions

- → **S7B Burnout** — when the expansion outpaces sustainable effort.
- → **S3E Ego Shock** — regression if a capability jump makes the person's contribution feel removable.
- → **S7V Evangelism** — lateral shift when the new identity becomes generative for others.

## ⚡ External triggers

⚡ Re-triggered by: **capability jumps** in the person's expanded domain, particularly ones that automate parts of what felt like the person's contribution.

## Healthiness

**Sustainable.** Growth is built into the state. Note the same assumption as S7M: S7I requires viable AI-collaborative work to remain in the expanded domain.

## Observation markers

- The person identifies themselves through capabilities that did not exist for them pre-AI.
- They describe AI as constitutive of what they can do, not as an external tool.
- They produce work in genres or domains they had no prior trained capability in.

## Open questions

- **Conjecture —** the distinction between S7M and S7I is internal — both look like "uses AI competently" from outside. Confirming the distinction requires self-report.
