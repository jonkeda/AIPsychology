# S7B — Burnout / Withdrawal

> *"I need a break from this."*

Step-back from AI use, temporary or permanent. Reachable from any S6 state and from sustained [S7I Identity Expansion](S7I-identity-expansion.md) or [S7V Evangelism](S7V-evangelism.md) intensity. A legitimate outcome, not a failure. Some users return after weeks or months at lower intensity; some don't return at all.

Distinguishable from emotional dropout (see [dropout.md](../dropout.md)) by trajectory: S7B follows a period of high engagement; emotional dropout often happens earlier, before significant engagement was ever achieved.

## Mechanism anchors

Conceptually related to **resource depletion** in the cognitive-control sense — sustained high-effort engagement, whether enthusiastic in S6E, anxious in S6D, driven in S6R, or socially compelled in S6S, draws on finite regulatory resources. Adjacent literature: cognitive-control fatigue research and the broader **burnout** literature in occupational psychology (Maslach), allowing for the unresolved debate about whether "depletion" is a literal energy mechanism or a motivation shift. Borrowed as vocabulary.

## Identity Stakes / Delegation modulation

S7B can follow any combination of Stakes and Delegation. The state itself is characterised by withdrawal from AI use, so Delegation Level effectively drops to D1 or to nothing.

## Transitions

- → **S5 Understanding** — return after recovery, often at lower intensity.
- → **S7M Maturity** — return with newly calibrated scope.
- → **Dropout** — the withdrawal becomes permanent.

## ⚡ External triggers

Triggered or accelerated by: **workload spikes**, **organisational changes**, **personal life events** that consume the same regulatory budget.

## Healthiness

**Recovery state.** A legitimate signal that something upstream broke. Living in S7B permanently means the cost of engagement outran the value. The state itself is not pathological; it is the resolution to a sustained imbalance.

## Observation markers

- AI use has dropped sharply from a previously high baseline.
- The person describes feeling depleted, cynical, or done with AI.
- Energy and engagement may also be down in adjacent areas of work.

## Open questions

- The distinction between temporary S7B and permanent dropout requires longitudinal observation.
