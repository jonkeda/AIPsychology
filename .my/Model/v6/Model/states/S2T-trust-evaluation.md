# S2T — Trust Evaluation

> *"Let me test this carefully before I commit."*

Non-defensive evaluation. Systematic testing on known problems, comparing AI output to personal output, evaluating reliability, calibrating expectations. Can convert to defensive evaluation if testing reveals a threat the person had not anticipated.

## Mechanism anchors

This state borrows from the **Technology Acceptance Model** (Davis, 1989) — adoption is driven by perceived usefulness and perceived ease of use. S2T is the active testing of both perceptions. Borrowed conceptually as vocabulary; the model does not commit to TAM as a causal account of S2T behavior.

Also conceptually related to **calibration under uncertainty** — the cognitive process of updating subjective probability estimates against observed evidence.

## Identity Stakes / Delegation modulation

S2T occupants typically have low or moderate Identity Stakes for the domain under test. The person is willing to update their view based on evidence because the evidence does not threaten core self-concept. If testing surfaces a capability that touches identity stakes the person had not anticipated, S2T can convert to [S2D Defensive Resistance](S2D-defensive-resistance.md).

Delegation Level during testing is usually D1 Information or D2 Task Execution. Higher delegation comes after trust is established.

## Transitions

- → **S5 Understanding** — trust established.
- → **S2D Defensive Resistance** — testing reveals a capability that newly threatens identity in a way the person did not anticipate going in. This is a different psychological event from "started S2D from the beginning" and the model describes it as a conversion, not a fork.
- → **Practical-flavoured Dropout** — fails quality or reliability standards. See [dropout.md](../dropout.md).

## ⚡ External triggers

No strong ⚡ marker. S2T is internally driven and is not reliably accelerated or reversed by named external events. Workplace mandates can shorten S2T but typically by skipping it rather than by accelerating its internal work.

## Healthiness

**Transitional only.** Healthy as a passage, harmful as a long residence. A person stuck in S2T for many months is usually facing a structural problem (no time, no real-world tasks to test on, blocked access to tools) rather than a psychological one.

## Observation markers

- Can describe both successes and failures of AI on known tasks.
- Stated view of AI has moved since they started testing.
- Has a deliberate test plan or evaluation routine.

The cleanest probe (see [observation-guide.md](../observation-guide.md)): "Tell me about a time AI did something that made you reconsider your view." An S2T person will have an answer.

## Open questions

- **Testable —** the asymmetric-updating signal that distinguishes S2T from S2D is observable across multiple evaluations but takes weeks to detect on a single subject.
