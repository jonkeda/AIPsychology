# S0 — Baseline

> *"AI is not a thing in my life right now."*

The absence of meaningful encounter. Listed for completeness and as the entry point for the trigger layer.

## Two readings of S0 Baseline

S0 applies to two distinct populations:

1. **Pre-encounter users.** People who have not yet had a substantive AI exposure but are likely to. For these users, S0 is transitional — S1 Initial Encounter is the next step.
2. **Permanent non-users.** People who have not used AI and will not. They occupy S0 as a terminal state, not a transitional one. Their work, life context, or personal preference keeps them outside the AI adoption trajectory.

The second population is **not** in [S2D Defensive Resistance](S2D-defensive-resistance.md) (which requires having considered and rejected AI on identity-threat grounds) and **not** in [S2P Pre-emptive Non-Adoption](S2P-pre-emptive-non-adoption.md) (which requires an active values-based rejection). They are simply outside the system. They have not formed a stance.

## Mechanism anchors

None. S0 is the default condition.

## Identity Stakes / Delegation modulation

Not applicable while in S0 itself. Identity Stakes becomes relevant at the moment of first encounter and shapes which fork is taken at S1.

## Transitions

- → **S1 Initial Encounter** — any substantive exposure event. Reading about AI does not move a person from S0; using or being asked to use it does.
- → (terminal) — for permanent non-users, S0 is the end of the model.

S0 → S1 is not inevitable. The model should not be read as predicting that everyone will eventually engage.

## ⚡ External triggers

- **Workplace mandate** — forced exposure that converts pre-encounter users to S1.
- **Peer adoption events** — informal exposure through close colleagues or family.
- **Compelling demos** — public events that convert pre-encounter users to S1 even without personal need.

## Healthiness

S0 is sustainable for permanent non-users in life contexts where AI is not relevant. It is transitional for pre-encounter users; staying in S0 is healthy until a real exposure event occurs.

## Observation markers

- Has not used AI tools in the last six to twelve months.
- Cannot describe any specific AI product from personal experience.
- Has not formed any defended stance about AI — neither defensive resistance nor ethical objection.

A practitioner using this model should be careful not to mistake permanent non-use for [S2D Defensive Resistance](S2D-defensive-resistance.md). The probe: ask whether the person has formed a stance about AI. S2D requires an active stance defended against evidence; permanent non-use requires no stance at all.

## Open questions

- What proportion of permanent non-users remain S0 throughout their working life remains unstudied.
- Whether passive media exposure (reading, watching, hearing about AI without using it) ever counts as enough to move S0 → S1 is undefined.
