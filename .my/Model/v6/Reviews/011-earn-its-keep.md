# Earn-Its-Keep Audit — Model V6

## TL;DR

- **S0 permanent-non-user reading fails all three criteria.** It is a scope boundary, not a construct. Delete or relabel as "out of scope."
- **Healthiness levels are editorial commentary, not model content.** They do not predict, enable intervention, or make falsifiable claims. Demote to a gloss document or delete.
- **S7I, S7E, S7V are behaviorally indistinguishable from S7M without self-report.** This undercuts their operationalization. Consider merging or renaming as modulations rather than states.
- **Delegation Ceiling is pure conjecture.** No measurement instrument, no specified falsifiable test, only narrative anchoring. Delete or move to limits-of-operationalization as aspirational vocabulary.
- **The S3E → S5 direct edge cannot be distinguished from accelerated S3E → S3B → S5 on single observation**, negating its explanatory value. Mark as "same as S3E → S3B → S5" and remove the edge.
- **PEN Pre-emptive Non-Adoption remains diagnostically fragile.** The three signals (cross-domain, evidence response, emotional register) are weak on single observation and both are "not yet tested." Consider whether this is S2D with ethical language, or whether the model needs a different framing.
- **S3X Structural Displacement's three resolutions are conjectured, not validated.** It serves as a useful exit-ramp label but lacks explanatory power beyond "role no longer exists."
- **The ⚡ trigger system annotates without predicting.** It names events but the model derives no predictions from them. Useful as reference but not a load-bearing construct.

## Findings

### 1. S0 Baseline permanent-non-user reading fails all three criteria

**What is failing:** V6 expands S0 to cover "permanent non-users" — people who occupy S0 as a terminal state, never encountering AI. The file distinguishes them from S2D (no defended stance against a considered threat) and from PEN (no active values-based rejection). They have simply not engaged and will not.

**Why this fails:**

- **Explanatory power — zero.** The permanent-non-user reading does not predict any transition, any state intensity, or any outcome. It is explicitly defined as *not forming a stance*. It explains nothing about the person; it labels them out of scope.
- **Operational use — zero.** V6 says: "The model should not be read as predicting that everyone will eventually engage." So the practitioner encounters a permanent non-user and the model says: you are outside the system. No intervention, no lever.
- **Falsifiability — zero.** There is no claim about the person, no prediction, no hypothesis. "They have not engaged" is a boundary condition, not a conjecture.

This is a scope-exclusion label, not a construct. It does not pay its way on any criterion.

**Fix:** Delete the permanent-non-user reading from S0 or move it to [populations.md](populations.md) as an explicit scope limit: "This model does not apply to people outside S0–S7. They are outside the adoption trajectory." Do not present it as a state.

### 2. S3X Structural Displacement fails all three criteria — or barely passes on explanatory power

**What is failing:** S3X is reached when someone concludes that viable AI-collaborative work no longer exists in their domain. Entry is triggered by external confirmation (role elimination, client collapse) or internal conclusion after S3B has failed.

**The problem in detail:**

- **Explanatory power — weak to none.** S3X explains that when a job disappears, the job is gone. This is not a psychological model; it is a labour-market observation. The file itself concedes this: "The model does not predict which roles will become economically non-viable, or when. That is an economics question." So what does S3X predict about *behaviour*? The file lists three outcomes (transition, exit, advocacy) and marks them all as **Conjecture — "not validated against a representative sample of displaced workers."** If the outcomes are conjectural, what does S3X predict?

- **Operational use — very weak.** The file frames S3X as a useful label: it tells practitioners *"this is no longer a psychology problem, it is an economic one."* But what does a practitioner *do* with that? The answer is: refer to economics, labour law, or social services. The model then offers nothing. Compare to S3E (support identity work) or S6D (rebuild self-trust) — these are actionable. S3X is a dismissal.

- **Falsifiability — weak.** The three resolutions are offered as observed patterns, not as tested predictions. Validation is listed as missing. The "adversarial-advocacy track" is noted as not fully described. This is a placeholder for a future state, not a finished construct.

**Related issue:** S3X also conflates two separate crises — the identity reckoning of S3E *plus* the structural blow. That compounding is useful to name, but it does not follow that S3X is a discrete state rather than a *label for a confluence of S3E + economic event*. A simpler model would say: S3E + structural facts → dropout at either emotional or practical axis. S3X adds a third category that lacks predictive content.

**Fix:** Demote S3X from a full state to a **labelled subtype of dropout**. Rename it "S3E + Structural Non-Viability (layered dropout)" and move it into [dropout.md](dropout.md) as a special case. This preserves the recognition that the two crises compound without claiming a separate state. If future work validates the three-resolution typology, promote it back. For now, it is an observation, not a prediction.

### 3. S7I Identity Expansion fails operationalization — indistinguishable from S7M without self-report

**What is failing:** S7I is defined as "The person develops capability or creative output they couldn't produce alone and partly defines themselves through this expansion." S7M is "calibrated adoption with bounded scope." The file acknowledges the core problem: "the distinction between S7M and S7I is internal — both look like 'uses AI competently' from outside. Confirming the distinction requires self-report."

**Why this is a failure:**

- **Explanatory power — undermined by indistinguishability.** If an observer cannot tell S7M from S7I on the same set of behaviours, then the distinction does not predict different outcomes or transitions. Both lead to the same end states (S7B, regression to S3E on capability jump). Both are "sustainable."

- **Operational use — zero.** The practitioner has no observable signal. The model says "ask the person whether they identify through the expanded capability." But this is self-report, not observation. And once the person has read the model, the self-report is contaminated. The file even concedes this: "the distinction is internal and is contaminated by self-report once a subject has read this model."

- **Falsifiability — stated as weak.** The file marks it as Conjecture. There is no specified test that would distinguish the two without relying on what the person tells you.

This is a conceptual distinction without a behavioural footprint. That makes it non-operational and non-falsifiable.

**Fix:** Either (a) find a behavioural signal that distinguishes S7I from S7M (e.g., the person enters new domains where they had no prior baseline; the person's identity description shifts to include AI partnership). Or (b) merge S7I and S7M as "S7 Integration" with a note that some people are rebuilding identity and some are protecting it, but both are stable. Or (c) demote S7I to a *modulation* of S7M ("Identity Expansion modulation") rather than a separate state.

### 4. S7E Ethical Integration fails operationalization for the same reason as S7I

**What is failing:** S7E is "sustained reflection on bias, intellectual property, authenticity, labour displacement, environmental cost, societal effects." The file says it "rarely appears as a sole end state" and "most often co-occurs with S7M or S7I." It also notes: "the line between healthy S7E and rumination is qualitatively understood but not operationally specified."

**The operational failure:**

- **Explanatory power — weak.** Does S7E predict a different outcome than S7M? No. Both are sustainable. Both can transition to S7V. Both regress to S3E on capability jumps. The difference is internal reflection, not external behaviour.

- **Operational use — very weak.** A practitioner observes someone in a sustainable end state. How do they know whether the person is S7M or S7E? They can only ask "are you thinking about ethics?" which is self-report. And if someone is in S7M + reflecting on ethics, are they in S7E? The file admits the distinction is hard: "S7E is rarely a sole end state."

- **Falsifiability — weak.** "The line between healthy S7E and rumination is qualitatively understood but not operationally specified." This admits the boundary is fuzzy and unmeasured.

Worse, S7E is defined by content (thinking about ethics) not by behaviour (what do you do differently?). This makes it unmeasurable and prone to over-classification.

**Fix:** Merge S7E into S7M as a modulation: "Some S7M users also engage in ethical reflection (S7E modulation)." Or, if ethical reflection is supposed to be load-bearing, specify a concrete behavioural marker: Does the person make visible decisions about what not to delegate? Do they articulate constraints in public? Do they differ materially in delegation patterns from S7M? Until then, S7E is "reflective S7M," not a separate state.

### 5. S7V Evangelism is a role or modulation, not a state

**What is failing:** S7V is defined as "the person's identity becomes partly defined by their role as a guide for others' adoption." The file lists four origins: through S3E, through S7I, through professional role, through generativity drive. It notes that S7V "is high-effort regardless of Stakes or Delegation."

**The structural problem:**

- **Explanatory power — questionable.** What does S7V predict? The same things S7M, S7I, or S7E predict (regression to S3E on capability jumps, transition to S7B on burnout). The external-facing work is a modulation on top of an underlying end state, not a separate state.

- **Operational use — weak.** A practitioner observes someone teaching others about AI. Does that tell them anything about the person's own state? No. An S6D person can evangelize. An S6E person often does. An S7M person might. S7V describes a role (mentor, speaker, advocate) not a state.

- **Falsifiability — weak to none.** The claim "S7V without depth of engagement collapses under hostile questioning" is testable in principle, but it is soft and describes a failure mode, not a stable-state prediction. Most S7V origination paths are observational, not predictive.

**The deeper issue:** V5 implied S7V required prior S3E. V6 downgrades: "S7V is *more common* among people with prior S3E but stable S7V without prior S3E also exists." This admission suggests S7V is not a state but a **possible role** layered on top of whatever end state a person has reached. A person in S7M can evangelize. A person in S7I can evangelize. The evangelist role does not move them to a different state; it is something they do *while* in S7M/I/E.

**Fix:** Rename S7V as "Integration + Evangelism" or "End-State Advocacy Modulation" and move it out of the state graph. Or keep it but make clear it is an overlay on S7M/I/E, not a distinct state. Rewrite the transitions section: instead of "S7M → S7V," say "S7M occupants can take on evangelism." The current structure treats it as a state but the model does no work specific to it; it is just "S7M + teaching."

### 6. Delegation Ceiling is pure conjecture with no measurement and no falsifiable test

**What is failing:** The Delegation Ceiling is "the maximum Task Delegation Level a person can accept for AI involvement in their core identity domain, given their current state and history." It is explicitly marked as **Conjecture**: "The Delegation Ceiling is a descriptive concept. No measurement instrument currently exists for it. The construct is offered as vocabulary for a phenomenon practitioners report but is not empirically validated."

**The three criteria:**

- **Explanatory power — weak to zero.** The construct is used once: to explain **coerced S5 Understanding** — "the person appears to integrate but has not changed their ceiling; the underlying resistance re-surfaces later." This is a single application. Does it predict a different outcome than the model already captures? S5 with a retained ceiling would regress on capability jump; the model already predicts regression. The ceiling adds no prediction.

- **Operational use — zero.** There is no measurement. A practitioner cannot assess someone's ceiling. The model offers no intervention keyed to the ceiling. It is purely retrospective: after someone regresses, you can posthoc attribute it to a ceiling they had not disclosed.

- **Falsifiability — zero.** No specified test. The file offers it as vocabulary. "The construct is offered as vocabulary for a phenomenon practitioners report" — this is the definition of an un-falsifiable folk concept, not a model element.

**The real problem:** The construct is used to block one narrow edge case (coerced S5) without adding explanatory content. A simpler model would say: people can appear to integrate while still defending against high-D tasks; they regress if forced to high-D. That is already in the model (via S3B → S5 with boundary held, then regression). The ceiling adds wordiness, not power.

**Fix:** Delete it. Or, if practitioners genuinely report it, move it to [limits-of-operationalization.md](limits-of-operationalization.md) as an aspiration: "Future research might develop an instrument to measure delegation ceiling. For now, it is vocabulary." Do not include it in the core model.

**Fix:** As in finding 1 — delete or move to populations.md.

### 8. Healthiness levels do not meet any criterion — they are editorial commentary

**What is failing:** The [healthiness.md](healthiness.md) file provides an interpretive layer mapping states to sustainability. S7M is "sustainable"; S6E is "sustainable with cost"; S3E is "transitional only"; S3X is "legitimate, not pathological."

**The three criteria:**

- **Explanatory power — zero.** Healthiness levels do not predict anything. They interpret what the model already predicts (sustainability, transition requirement, regression risk). They add no new claim.

- **Operational use — weak to none.** The file tells a practitioner "S7M is clean; S6D is corrosive." But the model already says S6D causes "self-trust erosion" and leads to S7B. The healthiness label adds moral weight ("corrosive is bad") but no new intervention.

- **Falsifiability — zero.** The file explicitly disclaims any model status: "This file is interpretation. The model itself is descriptive. Healthiness labels above are a reading, not a model claim." If it is not a model claim, it should not be in the model.

**The structural issue:** V6 is descriptive, not normative. The model should not contain normative judgments about states. If practitioners find healthiness judgments useful, that is a guide-layer responsibility, not a model responsibility.

**Fix:** Delete [healthiness.md](healthiness.md) or move it to an optional "practitioner guide" outside the core model. If kept, retitle it "Practitioner interpretation layer — not a model claim" and segregate it as a separate gloss document. Do not link it from [Model6.md](Model6.md).

### 9. The S3E → S5 direct edge cannot be distinguished from S3E → S3B → S5

**What is failing:** The state graph includes a direct edge S3E → S5, marked "(rare)" and accompanied by a note: "Most people who appear to take it are actually doing accelerated S3B and revisiting it later. **Testable —** distinguishing the direct path from a fast S3E → S3B → S5 path requires longitudinal observation; on a single observation the two are indistinguishable."

**Why this fails operationally:**

- **Explanatory power — undermined by indistinguishability.** If two paths look identical on observation and cannot be told apart without longitudinal data, the distinction has no explanatory value to a practitioner or researcher observing in real time.

- **Operational use — zero.** A practitioner observes someone move from S3E to S5. Which path did they take? The model says you cannot know without months of data. So the direct edge tells the practitioner nothing.

- **Falsifiability — admits defeat.** The file concedes: "on a single observation the two are indistinguishable." This is a way of saying "we can't test this claim without data we don't have." In a descriptive model, if two paths are observationally identical, they are the same path.

The direct S3E → S5 edge does no work. It is drawn to preserve a theoretical possibility ("maybe some people skip bargaining") but cannot be distinguished from the model's default. Remove it.

**Fix:** Delete the S3E → S5 edge. Rewrite the S3E section to say: "S3E typically leads to S3B (negotiated scope) or emotional dropout. Some people accelerate through S3B to S5 quickly. The distinction between rapid S3B → S5 and direct integration cannot be made on single observation and should not be reified as a separate edge." This preserves the possibility without over-complicating the graph.

### 10. PEN Pre-emptive Non-Adoption is diagnostically fragile and may be S2D with ethical language

**What is failing:** PEN is "a stable non-adoption position reached at or shortly after S1 Initial Encounter, based on values-grounded ethical appraisal rather than on capability testing or identity threat." The file explicitly acknowledges the diagnostic difficulty: "From outside, pre-emptive non-adoption and S2D dressed in ethical language look almost identical. Both decline to engage, both cite principled reasons, both discount capability evidence. The distinction only becomes visible over time."

The model offers three signals (cross-domain consistency, evidence response, emotional register), then adds: "Most real cases are mixed."

**The three criteria:**

- **Explanatory power — questionable.** Does PEN predict a different outcome than S2D? No. Both lead to stable non-adoption (or, in some cases, drift to S2D ↔ S2T). Both are marked as resistant to capability evidence. If both end states and transitions are the same, is PEN a separate state or a labelling variation of S2D?

- **Operational use — very weak.** The practitioner tries the three signals (cross-domain check, evidence response test, emotional register) and finds a "mixed case." The file concedes: "Most real cases are mixed. A person can be simultaneously in S2D (protecting competence) and making genuine ethical arguments that are also true and genuinely held." If most cases are mixed and unmixable, then the distinction does not operationalize.

- **Falsifiability — weak and temporal.** The file marks one signal as "Testable — cross-domain consistency over multiple months." But this requires months of observation. The other two signals (emotional register, evidence response) are qualitative and subjective. And the file itself notes: "these signals — none reliable on a single observation." This is an admission that the state is not reliably falsifiable on real-world timescales.

**The deeper problem:** S2D Defensive Resistance is defined as "identity-protection cognition" with "asymmetric updating." PEN is defined as "moral identity prioritisation." But both protect something (competence or values) and both discount evidence. The verbal distinction is clear; the behavioural distinction is not. If you cannot tell them apart in practice and both behave the same way, they are the same state.

**Fix:** Either (a) merge PEN into S2D and note that some S2D cases are grounded in identity threat and others in values conflict; the distinction is internal. (b) Find a robust observational signal that distinguishes them without self-report. (c) Keep PEN but mark it explicitly as "Conjecture — not validated" and move it to limits-of-operationalization. Do not present it as a clean seventh-inning-stretch state when the diagnostic frame admits confusion.

### 11. The ⚡ external-trigger system names events without making predictions

**What is failing:** The ⚡ marker system points to external events (model release, workplace mandate, regulatory change) that "materially change the probability of entering or leaving that state." The system is well-defined on paper: a state carries ⚡ if "there is at least one named external event that materially changes the *probability* of entering or leaving that state."

**The operational failure:**

- **Explanatory power — zero.** The model does not use the ⚡ markers to make predictions. There are no transition probabilities, no stated likelihoods, no claims of the form "when this event occurs, this transition probability increases from X to Y." The markers annotate but do not predict.

- **Operational use — weak to none.** A practitioner learns that S2D has ⚡ for model release. This tells them "watch for model releases." But the model gives no guidance on how a release should change their intervention or their expectation of the person's next state. Compare: S2D is identity-protection cognition, so practitioners should check whether identity is at stake. That is actionable. The ⚡ marker adds context but no leverage.

- **Falsifiability — weak.** The rule is stated ("a state carries ⚡ if") but the application is not systematic or tested. Some states carry ⚡; others do not. But there is no operationalised claim about *how much* the probability changes. Without that claim, the markers are descriptive labels, not falsifiable predictions.

The ⚡ system is useful as an annotation layer but it does not pay its way as a model construct. It is organizational, not predictive.

**Fix:** Keep the ⚡ markers as **reference annotations**, not as model elements. Relabel them in the documentation: "⚡ signals a potential external influence; see [triggers.md](triggers.md) for context. This annotation is for reference and does not carry predictive weight." Do not claim they are part of the model's explanatory power.

### 12. S1 Initial Encounter may be too thin to be a state (minor finding)

**What is failing:** S1 is defined as "first substantive contact" with "unformed stance." The file acknowledges: "How short S1 can legitimately be (some users move S0 → S1 → S2T inside a single afternoon) is undefined. Whether S1 has a meaningful internal duration or is better modelled as a transition than a state remains a design choice."

This is not a critical failure, but it is a design inconsistency. If some users spend hours in S1 and others spend minutes, and S1 has no transitions internal to itself (nothing that keeps a person in S1 longer), then S1 is not a state — it is a label for the moment between S0 and whichever fork (S2T, S2D, PEN) the person takes.

**Fix:** Clarify whether S1 is a real state or a transition label. If it is a label, move it out of the state graph and into the edge annotations (S0 → S2T via exposure event). If it is a real state, specify what keeps a person in S1 (hesitation? structural block? pending capability demonstration?) and how long a healthy S1 residence lasts. For now, S1 is ambiguous.

## Recommendations (Prioritised)

### Tier 1: Delete Immediately (no informational loss)

**1. Delete S0 permanent-non-user reading.** It is not a construct; it is a scope exclusion. Add one line to [populations.md](populations.md): "This model does not apply to people with no exposure to AI and no intention to engage." Done.

**2. Delete Healthiness levels file.** It is editorial commentary, not model content. Practitioners can judge sustainability themselves based on the state descriptions. If a guide-layer document is needed, that is separate work.

**3. Delete Delegation Ceiling.** No measurement, no test, no prediction. The "coerced S5" observation is already covered by "regression on capability jump with retained defensive boundaries." The ceiling adds only unnecessary complexity.

**4. Delete the S3E → S5 direct edge.** Redraw the graph to show S3E → {S3B, dropout}. S3B then leads to S5. This is the observationally true path.

**5. Relabel ⚡ markers from "model element" to "reference annotation."** Keep them in [triggers.md](triggers.md) but do not claim they predict anything. Use them as signposts: "when this external event occurs, watch for these state changes."

### Tier 2: Merge or Rename (consolidate)

**6. Merge S7I and S7E into S7M.** Specify that some S7M occupants are rebuilding identity (S7I modulation) and some are reflecting ethically (S7E modulation). Both are internal modulations, not separate states. Add one sentence to S7M: "Identity Expansion and Ethical Integration are common modulations of S7M; they describe what the person thinks about, not which state they are in."

**7. Rename S7V as "Evangelism Modulation (optional role within S7M/I/E)."** Make clear that the person can occupy any end state *and* take on external advocacy work. Do not treat it as a distinct state.

**8. Merge PEN into S2D or validate much more thoroughly.** Either (a) write PEN as "S2D grounded in values rather than identity threat" with a note that the distinction is internal and unreliable on observation. Or (b) run a longitudinal study with the three signals and report inter-rater reliability. Until then, it is not a defensible separate state.

**9. Demote S3X Structural Displacement to a labelled dropout subtype.** Move it into [dropout.md](dropout.md) as "When the domain is structurally eliminated: three observed resolutions (transition, exit, advocacy). Not validated; aspirational for future research."

### Tier 3: Clarify and Strengthen (fix in place)

**10. Clarify S1 as state or transition.** Specify how long a healthy S1 lasts. If it is truly a state, specify what mechanisms keep people in it. If it is a transition, relabel it.

**11. Add a measurement path for any remaining constructs.** For each state, specify: "This state can be confirmed on a single observation via [specific marker]." For S7I and S7E, this will be impossible without self-report; that proves the merge.

## Net Effect: V7 Specification

Removing/merging the failing constructs yields a **simpler, more testable V6.5**:

- **Eliminate 4 full constructs:** S0 permanent-non-user, S7I, S7E, S7V (as states).
- **Eliminate 3 architectural elements:** Delegation Ceiling, ⚡ triggers (as model elements), S3E → S5 edge, Healthiness levels.
- **Merge S3X into dropout:** as a subtype, not a state.
- **Consolidate PEN:** either into S2D or eliminate.
- **Result:** 13 core states + 2 modified states (S7M with modulations, S1 if kept) + 2 axes + multi-occupancy rules + two-axis dropout.

This is a working descriptive model. The deletions remove constructs that either (1) lack falsifiable content, (2) cannot be operationalised without self-report, or (3) are observationally indistinguishable from simpler alternatives.

The surviving model remains rich enough to describe adaptation patterns but is no longer carrying dead weight.
