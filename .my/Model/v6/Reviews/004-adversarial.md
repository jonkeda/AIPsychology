# 004-adversarial.md

## TL;DR

- V6's rename from "mechanism anchors" to "related literature" lowers the evidentiary bar rather than raising it. Vocabulary connections are unfalsifiable; mechanism claims at least have shape.
- The "descriptive-first" stance is framed as epistemic humility but functions as immunity from testing. You make claims about transitions without claiming to predict them.
- S3X Structural Displacement is a labeled subset of dropout (the practical-severity-extreme case), not a new state. PEN Pre-emptive Non-Adoption is S2D Defensive Resistance with genuine values mixed in, rarely distinguishable without months of observation.
- Per-domain identity-stakes scoping is positioned as refinement but is an unfalsifiable rescue clause. When the model misfires, redeclare the identity stakes in that domain post-hoc.
- These three features together armor the model against falsification: redefine concepts as vocabulary, declare the model descriptive-not-predictive, redeclare domain parameters. No amount of evidence can disconfirm it.

## Findings

### 1. The rename to "related literature" trades falsifiable claims for tautologies — this is retreat, not honesty

V5 anchored each state to a mechanism. S3E Ego Shock (the identity-disruption state) was anchored to self-efficacy collapse and terror management — claims you could test by measuring self-efficacy beliefs in S3E people and seeing if they collapsed. If they didn't, the anchor was wrong.

V6 renames this to "related literature" and adds a disclaimer in [Model6.md](Model6.md): "Knowing that S3E Ego Shock is conceptually related to self-efficacy collapse tells the reader what kind of phenomenon S3E belongs to. It does not let anyone diagnose S3E in a person, and it does not commit the model to the claim that self-efficacy collapse is the cause of S3E."

Here is what happened. The model stopped making falsifiable claims and started making definitional ones. A "vocabulary connection" is tautological. S3E is identity-disruption. Self-efficacy collapse is a form of identity-related disruption. So of course they are related — they are the same thing under different names. The name does no work. You could say S3E is related to dissonance, or to existential threat, or to any other concept that sounds like identity disruption, and it would be equally true and equally uninteresting.

The claim that this is more honest than V5 is wrong. It is less honest. V5 said "here is the mechanism; test it." V6 says "here is a vocabulary tag; it commits to nothing." That is not transparency; that is obfuscation dressed as transparency.

**Fix:** Choose a lane. Either:
- **(a) Make the literature anchors falsifiable:** "S3E shows the characteristic updating pattern of identity-protection cognition (Kahan, 2017). A test: measure asymmetric evidence updating in people in S3E across multiple releases. If evidence updates symmetrically, the anchor is wrong."
- **(b) Drop the literature entirely:** Name the states plainly. S3E is identity disruption. Describe it behaviorally. The conceptual connection to self-efficacy is interesting but not necessary to understanding the state.

Don't do what V6 does: claim conceptual grounding while disclaiming any commitment to what the grounding means.

### 2. The "descriptive-first" declaration gives the model immunity from criticism it doesn't deserve

V6 opens by declaring itself descriptive: "This model is descriptive. It provides names for recognisable psychological patterns. It does **not** provide causal mechanisms. Empirical predictions. Diagnostic instruments. Treatment protocols."

Then it immediately describes transitions as if they are observed patterns. S2D Defensive Resistance typically leads to S3E Ego Shock. S3E typically leads to S3B Bargaining or S5 Understanding. S1 Initial Encounter forks to either S2T Trust Evaluation or S2D, depending on Identity Stakes.

These are claims about how people move through states. They are not *predictions* of what will happen next, so the "descriptive-not-predictive" shield is rhetorical cover. The model describes transitions, but you cannot test whether the transitions are real because the model does not commit to predicting them. This is a classic defensive move.

Example: someone in S2D goes straight to S5 Understanding, skipping S3E. V5 would say "the model predicted wrong." V6 says "the model is descriptive, not predictive, so this observation is not a disconfirmation." But then the model still contains the claim that S2D typically leads to S3E. That claim is either true, false, or untestable. V6 will not commit to which.

The "described patterns" in the state graph function exactly like predictions to any reader using the model. The epistemic humility of the label gives immunity without any of the cost of actually being humble about the claims.

**Fix:** Make one choice:
- **(a) Predictive:** Commit to the transitions as claims that could be wrong. Specify a study that could falsify them. ("A longitudinal panel of 100+ people tracked across three model releases, measured at each release on state location and transition. We test whether S2D→S3E is the most common path (hypothesis: >60% of S2D people enter S3E within 12 months).")
- **(b) Genuinely descriptive:** Stop making claims about which states lead to which. Instead: "In observed trajectories, we have seen people move from S2D to S3E, and also from S2D to S3B, and also from S2D directly to S5. Here are three example profiles." No implied frequency; no "typically."

Marking things Conjecture or Testable does not solve this. You have not made them falsifiable; you have just flagged that they are not.

### 3. S3X Structural Displacement is a labeled exit path, not a state — it is a flavour of dropout with a clearer economic reason

V6 introduces S3X (Structural Displacement) as a state reached when a person concludes "my job is not being disrupted, my job is being replaced." The file distinguishes it from S3E Ego Shock (identity question) and from dropout (willing exit).

But read the logic. S3X is reached when "viable AI-collaborative work no longer exists in the person's domain." That is, when they conclude there is nowhere to adapt *to*. The identity reckoning is still unresolved, but it is now joined by an economic crisis: "the answer is that my economic position is gone, and now what?"

Here is the problem. This is not a new state. This is a path through the dropout plane that the existing model already describes. Look at the [dropout.md](dropout.md) file. Dropout happens on two axes: practical severity (tool failures, workflow friction) and emotional severity (identity threat, exhaustion). A dropout can be high practical, high emotional, low practical, high emotional, etc.

S3X is the case where practical severity is extreme because the labour market has objectively eliminated the role. It is not a "person is unwilling to continue" situation (emotional dropout). It is a "person has no position to continue into" situation. That is just practical-severity-extreme on the dropout plane. The fact that the practical severity has a clear structural reason rather than a tool-failure reason does not make it a state.

The S3X file says: "S3X is not emotional dropout. Emotional dropout happens when the psychological cost exceeds what the person is willing to pay. S3X is reached when that domain is gone." But this is a distinction between the *reason* for exiting, not between psychological states. Both are exits. One has an economic cause, one has a psychological cause.

The model has relabeled a dropout scenario as a state to make itself look more comprehensive. This is not an advance.

**Fix:** Demote S3X from state to a documented path. Write it in the [dropout.md](dropout.md) file as: "Some people drop out because their labour market has made the choice for them — the role is economically non-viable and there is no position to adapt into. This is a high-practical-severity dropout, but with a clear structural cause rather than a tool-failure cause. The psychological work is the same as other dropouts (accepting the exit, finding what comes next), but the blame and timeline differ."

Or commit to an operational test that distinguishes S3X from practical-high dropout without using the tautology "S3X is when the role is gone." That test does not exist in V6.

### 4. PEN Pre-emptive Non-Adoption is S2D Defensive Resistance with genuine values mixed in — the model admits it cannot reliably separate them

V6 introduces PEN as a state: values-grounded non-adoption at first encounter, distinct from S2D (identity-protection cognition).

The diagnostic file [PEN-pre-emptive-non-adoption.md](states/PEN-pre-emptive-non-adoption.md) lists three signals: cross-domain consistency (does the ethical objection apply consistently?), evidence response (does the position hold if the concern is addressed?), emotional register (is it moral conviction or anxiety?).

Then it says: "Most real cases are mixed. A person can be simultaneously in S2D (protecting competence) and making genuine ethical arguments that are also true and genuinely held... Labeling a mixed case as 'pure PEN' would be wrong; so would 'pure S2D.' Use the frame to understand the mixture, not to classify it."

So the model has introduced a state and then immediately admitted that in most cases you cannot tell whether someone is in that state or in S2D Defensive Resistance. The signals require longitudinal observation across months and multiple domains. The file then explicitly says "don't classify people as pure PEN, use it to understand mixtures."

This is not a state. This is a footnote in the S2D file. A state is something you can identify. PEN, as described, is only distinguishable from S2D after months of observation of cross-domain behavior and evidence response — the exact things the model said it would not require.

What V6 has actually done is add a label that covers "S2D plus genuine values reasoning." That is not a new state; that is a recognition that S2D people sometimes have real moral objections alongside their identity-protection cognition. This is always true. You do not need a new state to describe it; you need a note about heterogeneity within S2D.

**Fix:** Demote PEN to an annotation in [S2D-defensive-resistance.md](states/S2D-defensive-resistance.md). Write: "Some people in S2D have genuine, values-grounded ethical objections to AI use alongside their identity-protection cognition. These cases are hard to distinguish from 'pure ethical non-adoption' without months of longitudinal observation. The three signals (cross-domain consistency, evidence response, emotional register) are useful for practitioners to understand the mixture. If the evidence response pattern suggests the values are doing the real work, prioritize the ethical conversation over the identity-protection work."

Do not give it a state number. Do not put it in the graph. It is heterogeneity within S2D, not a separate state.

### 5. Per-domain identity-stakes scoping is billed as a refinement but functions as an unfalsifiable rescue clause

V6 makes explicit something V5 implied: "Identity Stakes is **per-domain**, not per-person." The same person can be S2D Defensive Resistance (identity-threatened) in their core domain and S5 Understanding (stable, integrated) in a peripheral one.

This is accurate as a description of human psychology. People are psychologically flexible. But it creates a logical problem for the model: identity stakes are now unmeasurable in advance.

The model's core claim is: "high-stakes people tend to fork to S2D; low-stakes people fork to S2T." But identity stakes are defined post-hoc by observing which fork the person takes. High stakes = person went to S2D. Low stakes = person went to S2T. This is circular. The parameter is defined by the outcome it is supposed to explain.

Example: you predict someone will go to S2T because you have judged their identity stakes low. They go to S2D. Instead of the model being wrong, you declare: "actually, the identity stakes in this domain were higher than I thought." The model is now unfalsifiable. Every surprise observation can be reframed as a revelation about previously-unmeasured domain parameters.

V5 had the same per-domain structure but kept it quieter, which was more honest. V6 elevated it to a design principle, making it look like a feature when it is really an escape hatch.

**Fix:** Define identity stakes independently of behavior. Propose a measurement that works before you observe which fork someone takes. Examples that would work:
- "Identity stakes = years invested in training / certification in this domain"
- "Identity stakes = % of income from this domain"
- "Identity stakes = qualitative interview on how central this skill is to self-concept"

Then run a prospective study. If low-stakes people still go to S2D Defensive Resistance at rates >40%, the model's fork prediction is wrong and needs revision.

If you cannot define stakes in advance, the per-domain framing is not a model feature; it is an admission that the model's core dichotomy (S2T vs S2D fork) may not be real.

### 6. The compound effect of three escape hatches makes the model unfalsifiable as a whole

Put the pieces together. The model:

1. Uses vocabulary anchors that commit to nothing ("related literature" can mean any conceptually nearby construct).
2. Declares itself descriptive-not-predictive while describing transitions as typical patterns.
3. Allows per-domain identity-stakes redefinition post-hoc.

The result: no amount of observation can disconfirm the model. Here is why:

- Observation contradicts a transition? The transition was never a prediction; the model is descriptive.
- Observation contradicts a state definition? Reframe the state as "related to" a different vocabulary construct.
- Observation contradicts the fork prediction (low stakes should S2T)? Redeclare identity stakes for that domain.

This is not a model of psychological adaptation. This is a vocabulary set with three layers of insulation against criticism. Kübler-Ross's five stages of grief faced the exact same problem (people do not actually progress through five ordered stages), and the response was to make the model more specific and testable, or to admit the model is loose. V6 chose a third path: add escape hatches that prevent the model from ever being obviously wrong.

**Fix:** Commit to falsifiability or admit the model is speculative. Pick one:

- **(a) Make it falsifiable:** Lock down the measurement of identity stakes in advance. Commit to specific transition probabilities (or ranges). Run longitudinal studies with pre-registered hypotheses. Accept that the model could be wrong.
- **(b) Admit it is speculative:** Reframe the model as "a vocabulary and an orientation, useful for practitioners to think about adaptation patterns, but not yet grounded in evidence. This is a first draft of how to think about this space, not a tested description of it." This is actually more honest.

The current position — "this is descriptive so it is safe from criticism, but here are all the patterns anyway" — is untenable.

## Minimum Changes to Make This Critique Not Apply

If the author wants to keep the model, these are the changes that would make the critique no longer stick:

1. **Operationalize Identity Stakes** — provide a measurement that does not depend on observing which fork the person takes. (Survey item, income data, qualitative interview, time-in-domain.) Then run a prospective validation study. If low-stakes people still show S2D rates >40%, revise the fork.

2. **Choose between "mechanism" and "vocabulary"** — either restore falsifiable mechanism claims and commit to testing them, or drop the literature anchors and describe states purely behaviorally. Do not claim vocabulary anchors while running from empirical commitment.

3. **Make transition claims testable or drop them** — either specify a study that would falsify "S2D typically leads to S3E," or stop claiming that S2D typically leads anywhere. "In observed trajectories we have seen X, Y, Z" is defensible; "typically leads to" is a prediction that needs a test.

4. **Demote S3X and PEN from states** — reframe them as documented paths (S3X as a structured-practical-severity dropout variant, PEN as heterogeneity within S2D) or operationalize them with pre-observation diagnostics that do not require months of longitudinal data.

5. **Add falsifiability test cases to limits-of-operationalization** — for each of the three escape hatches (vocabulary redefinition, descriptive-not-predictive shield, per-domain parameter redeclare), specify what observation would show the escape hatch is being misused. You cannot prevent rescue clauses; you can make their overuse visible.
