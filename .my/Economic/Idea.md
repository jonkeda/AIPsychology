#### Option 4 — Companion model: AI Economic Displacement Cycle (maximal)

Write a second document. The existing model stays focused on psychological adaptation; a companion model describes the economic-displacement trajectory (warning signs, transition periods, viability assessment, retraining pathways, advocacy positions). Cross-link them. Most real situations involve both, so practitioners use them together.

**What it buys:** Both problems get the depth they deserve. Neither model gets distorted by trying to cover the other.
**What it costs:** Two models to maintain. Clear scope boundary needed between them. Risk that readers use only one and miss the other half of the picture. Significantly more work.
**When this is the right choice:** If displacement is going to be a major focus and there is appetite for a second document.
